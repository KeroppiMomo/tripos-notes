• Identity: since $H$ is non-empty, there exists some $x\in H$. Note that $$ e = x\cdot x^{-1} \in H. $$

  • Inverse: also for all $h\in H$, $$ h^{-1} = e\cdot h^{-1} \in H. $$

  • Closure: for all $a, b\in H$, by above $b^{-1}$ exists. So $$ a\cdot b = a\cdot (b^{-1})^{-1} \in H. $$

  • Associativity follows from $G$ being a group.
