Clearly $\phi^{-1}$ is bijective.

For any $h_1, h_2\in H$, $$\begin{align*} \phi\big(\phi^{-1}(h_1)\cdot \phi^{-1}(h_2)\big) &= \phi\big(\phi^{-1}(h_1)\big)\cdot \phi\big(\phi^{-1}(h_1)\big) \\ &= h_1\cdot h_2 \end{align*}$$ Therefore $$ \phi^{-1}(h_1)\cdot \phi^{-1}(h_2) = \phi^{-1}(h_1\cdot h_2),$$ so $\phi^{-1}$ is an homomorphism.

Hence $\phi^{-1}$ is an isomorphism.
