Let $G$ be the isometry group of tetrahedron and $G_0\leq G$ be the index-2 subgroup of rotational symmetries.

By orbit-stabiliser, under the action of $G_0$, the orbit of a face has size 4, and the stabiliser of a face has size 3 since each face is a triangle. Therefore $$ |G_0| = 4\times 3 = 12. $$ Hence $$ |G| = 2\times 12 = 24. $$

Now note that the action on the 4 vertices defines a homomorphism $$ \theta: G \to S_4, $$ which, by the four-point lemma, has kernel $\set{\mrm{Id}}$ and is therefore injective.

Since $|G| = |S_4| = 24$, $\theta$ is an isomorphism, so $$ G\isom S_4. $$

By the uniqueness of $A_4$, $$ G_0 \isom A_4. $$
