Definition (conjugation) open_in_new

Let $G$ be a group and $g\in G$. For all $h\in G$, the element $$ hgh^{-1} \in G $$ is called the conjugate of $g$ by $h$.

Examples of conjugations open_in_new

  • If $G$ is abelian, then for any $g,h\in G$, $$ hgh^{-1} = hh^{-1} g = g $$ so the only conjugate of $g$ is $g$ itself.

  • In $D_{2n}$, consider the conjugate of $s$ by $r$. By the dihedral relation, $$\begin{align*} r^{-1} s &= sr \\ r^2r^{-1} s r^{-1} &= r^2 s r r^{-1}\\ rsr^{-1} &= r^2 s \end{align*}$$ More generally, $$ r^k s r^{-k} = r^{2k} s. $$ What reflection is this? Note that $$\begin{align*} (r^{2k} s)(e^{2\pi ik/n}) &= (r^ksr^{-k})(e^{2\pi ik/n}) \\ &= (r^k s)(1) \\ &= r^k(1) \\ &= e^{2\pi ik/n} \end{align*}$$ so $r^{2k} s$ fixes $e^{2\pi ik/n}$ and therefore reflects about the line of symmetry through $e^{2\pi ik/n}$.

Proposition (conjugation as an action) open_in_new

$G$ acts on $G$ by conjugation: $$ g(\gamma) = g\gamma g^{-1}. $$

Definition (conjugacy class, centraliser) open_in_new

  • The orbit of $\gamma$ under the conjugation action is called the conjugacy class of $\gamma$, $$ \mrm{ccl}(\gamma) = \sb{g\gamma g^{-1}}{g\in G}. $$ This is the set of all conjugates of $\gamma$.

  • The stabiliser of $\gamma$ under the conjugation action is called the centraliser of $\gamma$, $$ C_G(\gamma) = \sb{g\in G}{g\gamma g^{-1} = \gamma}. $$ This is set of all elements in $G$ that commute with $\gamma$, i.e. $g\gamma = \gamma g$.

Definition (centre of a group) open_in_new

The centre of a group $G$ is the set of elements that commute with all elements in $G$, $$ Z(G) = \sb{h\in G}{gh = hg\ \forall g\in G} = \bigcap_{g\in G}C_G(g).$$

The centre is precisely the union of the conjugacy classes of size 1.
