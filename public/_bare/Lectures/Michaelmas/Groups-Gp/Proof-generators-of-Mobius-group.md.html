For any $\mu\in\mathcal M$, let $$\begin{align*} z_1 &= \mu(0) \\ z_2 &= \mu(1) \\ z_3 &= \mu(\infty). \end{align*}$$

  1. Step 1: sending $z_3$ to $\infty$

We want to construct $\mu_1$ such that $\mu_1(z_3) = \infty$.

  • If $z_3=\infty$, then let $\mu_1 = \mrm{Id}$.

  • Otherwise, let $\mu_1(z) = 1/(z - z_3) = \gamma\circ\beta_{-z_3}(z)$.

Now let $z_1' = \mu_1(z_1)$ and $z_2' = \mu_2(z_2)$.

  1. Step 2: sending $z_1$ to $0$

To achieve this goal, let $\mu_2(z) = z - z_1' = \beta_{-z_1'}(z)$. Then we have $$\begin{align*} \mu_2\circ\mu_1(z_3) &= \infty \\ \mu_2\circ\mu_1(z_1) &= 0. \end{align*}$$

Now let $z_2'' = \mu_2\circ\mu_1(z_2)$.

  1. Step 3: sending $z_2$ to $1$

Now we let $\mu_3(z) = z/z_2'' = \alpha_{1/z_2''}(z)$.

Then we have $$\begin{align*} \mu_3\circ\mu_2\circ\mu_1(z_1) &= 0, \\ \mu_3\circ\mu_2\circ\mu_1(z_2) &= 1, \\ \mu_3\circ\mu_2\circ\mu_1(z_3) &= \infty. \end{align*}$$

Therefore $(\mu_3\circ\mu_2\circ\mu_1)\inv$ and $\mu$ agree on $0,1,\infty$ and hence everywhere by the three-point lemma.
