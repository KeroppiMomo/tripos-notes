For any $m\in\mathbb Z$, note that for $n=0$, $$\begin{align*} a^m\cdot a^n &= a^m\cdot a^0 \\ &= a^m\cdot e &&\text{by definition} \\ &= a^m. \end{align*}$$ Assume $a^m\cdot a^n = a^{m+n}$ is true for $n=k$ for some non-negative integer $k$. For $n=k+1$, $$\begin{align*} a^m\cdot a^{k+1} &= a^m\cdot (a^k\cdot a) &&\text{by definition} \\ &= (a^m\cdot a^k)\cdot a \\ &= a^{m+k}\cdot a. \end{align*}$$

  • If $m+k\geq 0$, then by definition $a^{m+k}\cdot a = a^{m+k+1}$.

  • If $m+k=0$, then $a^{m+k}\cdot a = e\cdot a = a = a^{m+k+1}$.

  • Otherwise if $m+k<0$, then $$\begin{align*} a^{m+k}\cdot a &= (a^{-1})^{-(m+k)}\cdot a &&\text{by definition} \\ &= \big((a^{-1})^{-(m+k)-1}\cdot a^{-1}\big)\cdot a &&\text{by definition} \\ &= (a^{-1})^{-(m+k+1)}\cdot (a^{-1}\cdot a) \\ &= a^{m+k+1}\cdot e &&\text{by definition} \\ &= a^{m+k+1}. \end{align*}$$

Hence $a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}$ for all $m,n\in\mathbb Z$ with $n\geq 0$.

Assume $a^m\cdot a^n=a^{m+n}$ is true for $n=-k$ for some non-negative integer $k$. For $n=-(k+1)$, $$\begin{align*} a^m\cdot a^{-(k+1)} &= a^m\cdot (a^{-1})^{k+1} &&\text{by definition} \\ &= a^m\cdot \big((a^{-1})^k\cdot (a^{-1})\big) &&\text{by definition} \\ &= \big(a^m\cdot (a^{-1})^k\big)\cdot a^{-1} \\ &= \big(a^m\cdot a^{-k})\cdot a^{-1} &&\text{by definition and }(a^{-1})^0=a^{-0}=e \\ &= a^{m-k}\cdot a^{-1} \end{align*}$$

  • If $m+k>0$, then $a^{m-k}\cdot a^{-1} = a^{m-k-1}\cdot a\cdot a^{-1} = a^{m-k-1}$.

  • If $m+k=0$, then $a^{m+k}\cdot a^{-1} = e\cdot a^{-1} = a^{-1} = a^{m+k-1}$.

  • Otherwise if $m+k<0$, then $$\begin{align*} a^{m-k}\cdot a^{-1} &= (a^{-1})^{-(m-k)}\cdot a^{-1} &&\text{by definition} \\ &= (a^{-1})^{-(m-k)+1} &&\text{by definition} \\ &= (a^{-1})^{-(m-k-1)} \\ &= a^{m-k-1} &&\text{by definition} \end{align*}$$

Hence $a^m\cdot a^n = a^{m+n}$ for all $m,n\in\mathbb Z$ with $n\leq 0$.

Therefore, $a^m\cdot a^n=a^{m+n}$ for all $m,n\in\mathbb Z$.
