Let $\pmat{i&j}$ be a transposition with $j>i$. We will prove by induction on $j-i$.

Base case: for $j=i+1$, it is already a product of one adjacent transposition aka itself.

Inductive step: for $j>i+1$, then note that $$ \pmat{i&j} = \pmat{j-1&j} \pmat{i&j-1} \pmat{j-1&j} $$ and the results follows by induction.
