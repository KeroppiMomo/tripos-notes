To prove the forward direction, let $$\sigma_1 = \pmat{a^1_1 & a^1_2 & \ldots & a^1_{k_1}} \ldots \pmat{a^l_1 & a^l_2 & \ldots & a^l_{k_l}}$$ be disjoint cycles. Note that if $\tau\in S_n$ sends $$ \tau: a^i_j \mapsto b^i_j, $$ then $$ \tau\sigma\tau\inv(b^i_j) = \tau\sigma(a^i_j) = \tau(a^i_{j +_{k_i} 1}) = b^i_{j +_{k_i} 1} $$ (where $+_{k_i}$ denotes addition mod $k_i$), and any other elements fixed by $\sigma_1$ is automatically fixed by $\tau\sigma\tau\inv$. So $$ \tau\sigma\tau\inv = \pmat{b^1_1 & b^1_2 & \ldots & b^1_{k_1}} \ldots \pmat{b^l_1 & b^l_2 & \ldots & b^l_{k_l}},$$ as required.

To prove the backward direction, suppose $$ \sigma_2 = \pmat{b^1_1 & b^1_2 & \ldots & b^1_{k_1}} \ldots \pmat{b^l_1 & b^l_2 & \ldots & b^l_{k_l}}.$$ The above construction provides $\tau$ such that $\sigma_2 = \tau\sigma_1\tau\inv$.
