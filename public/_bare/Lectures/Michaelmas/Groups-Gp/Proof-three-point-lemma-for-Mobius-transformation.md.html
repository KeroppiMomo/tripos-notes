The idea of the proof is that the equation $z = \frac{az+b}{cz+d}$ is quadratic in $z$, so 3 distinct solutions mean that the equation is zero.

Let $\mu(z) = \frac{az+b}{cz+d}$ and $w_1, w_2, w_3$ be the fixed points. A fixed point $w_i$ satisfies the equation $$ w_i = \frac{aw_i + b}{cw_i + d}. $$

If one of $w_i$, WLOG say $w_1$, equals $\infty$, then $c=0$. Then $w_2, w_3\in\C$ satisfies $$ w_i = \frac{aw_i + b}{d}, $$ which is equivalent to $$(a-d)w_i + b = 0.$$ This linear equation has two distinct solutions only if $$ a=d, \quad b=0. $$ Therefore $$ \mu(z) = \frac{az + 0}{0z + a} = z. $$

Now suppose none of $w_i=\infty$. Then $w_1,w_2,w_3\in\C$ satisfies $$ w_i = \frac{aw_i + b}{cw_i + d}, $$ which is equivalent to $$ cw_i^2 + (d-a)w_i - b = 0. $$ This is a quadratic equation but with 3 distinct solutions. Therefore $$ c=0,\quad d=a,\quad b=0. $$ By the same logic we have $\mu(z) = z$ as required.
