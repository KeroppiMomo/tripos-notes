Alternatively:

Since $g=ge\in gH$, we need to show each $g\in G$ is in at most one $\gamma H$.

Note that $$\begin{align*} g \in \gamma H &\iff g = \gamma h && h\in H \\ &\iff g^{-1} = h^{-1} \gamma^{-1} \\ &\iff g^{-1} = h' \gamma^{-1} && h'\in H \\ &\iff g^{-1} \in H\gamma^{-1} \end{align*}$$

Therefore $$\begin{align*} &g\in \gamma H\cap \gamma'H \\ \implies& g \in H\gamma^{-1} \cap H\gamma'^{-1} \\ \implies& H\gamma^{-1} = H\gamma'^{-1} &&\text{right cosets partition } H \\ \implies& \gamma H = \gamma'H \end{align*}$$
