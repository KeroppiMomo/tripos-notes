Define the map $$\begin{align*} \Phi: && \gl 2\C &\to \mathcal M \\ && \pmat{a&b\\c&d} &\mapsto \Big(z\mapsto \frac{az + b}{cz+d}\Big). \end{align*}$$ This is well-defined since $ad-bc\neq 0$ required in $\mathcal M$ is also imposed in $\gl 2\C$.

$\Phi$ is a homomorphism by computation of composition in $\mathcal M$.

Clearly $\Phi$ is surjective.

A matrix $\pmat{a & b \\ c&d} \in \ker\Phi$ iff the map $z\mapsto\dfrac{az+b}{cz+d}$

  • fixes 0, so $b=0$;

  • fixes $\infty$, so $c=0$; and

  • fixes 1, so $a+b=c+d$ and hence $a=d$.

Therefore $\ker\Phi = \C_\times$.

The result follows from the isomorphism theorem.
