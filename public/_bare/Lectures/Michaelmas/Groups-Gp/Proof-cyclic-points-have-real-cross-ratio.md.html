By the definition of cross-ratio, we have $\alpha\in\mathcal M$ where $\alpha$ sends $$\begin{align*} z_1 &\mapsto 0 \\ z_2 &\mapsto 1 \\ z_3 &\mapsto \infty \\ z_4 &\mapsto [z_1,z_2,z_3,z_4]. \end{align*}$$

Forward direction: if $z_1,z_2,z_3,z_4$ lie on a circle $C$, then since $\alpha$ preserves circles, $0,1,\infty,\alpha(z_4)\in\alpha(C)$ which is a circle. The only circle that passes through $0,1,\infty$ is $\R\cup\set\infty$, so $\alpha(z_4) = [z_1,z_2,z_3,z_4] \in \R\cup\set\infty$.

Backward direction: if $\alpha(z_4)\in\R\cup\set\infty$, then $0,1,\infty,\alpha(z_4)\in\R\cup\set\infty$ which is a circle. Then since $\alpha^{-1}\in\mathcal M$ preserves circles, $z_1, z_2, z_3, z_4\in\alpha^{-1}(\R\cup\set\infty)$ which is also a circle.
