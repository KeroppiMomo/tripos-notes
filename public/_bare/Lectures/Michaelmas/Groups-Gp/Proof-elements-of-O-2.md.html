By the previous reflection, let $A = S_{v_1} \ldots S_{v_k}$ where $k\leq 2$.

  • If $A\not\in \mrm{SO}(2)$, then $\det A = -1$. Therefore $k$ is odd so $k=1$.

  • If $A\in\mrm{SO}(2)$, then $k=0$ or $2$. Unless $A=I$, we have $k=2$ and $A=S_uS_v$ where $S_u\neq S_v$. $P_u$ and $P_v$ only intersect at $0$, so for $x\neq 0$, suppose $$\begin{align*} S_uS_v x &= x \\ S_v x &= S_u x \neq x \end{align*}$$ But then $$ u \parallel x-S_u x = x - S_v x \parallel v$$ so $S_u = S_v$, which is a contradiction. $\mrm{Fix}(A) = \set 0$ as required.

Alternative proof of $A\in\mrm{SO}(2)$ implies rotation using explicit rotation formula:

Let $A\in\mrm{SO}(2)$. Since columns of $A$ are orthonormal, $$ A = \pmat{a & \pm b \\ -b & \pm a} $$ and since $\det A = 1$, $$ \pm a^2 \pm b^2 = 1 $$ so we can only pick +. By setting $a = \cos\theta$ and $b = \sin\theta$, $$ A = \pmat{\cos\theta & \sin\theta \\ -\sin\theta & \cos\theta }, $$ which is the rotation matrix.
