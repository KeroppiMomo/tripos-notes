Let's denote $S = \mrm{Stab}_G(x)$ and $$ \phi: gS \mapsto gx. $$

First, $\phi$ is well-defined because suppose $g_1 S = g_2 S$ for some $g_1,g_2\in G$. Then $$ g_1 = g_1 e \in g_1 S = g_2 S $$ so there exists $s\in S$ such that $$ g_1 = g_2 s.$$ Therefore $$\begin{align*} \phi(g_1 S) &= g_1 x \\ &= (g_2 s)x \\ &= g_2(sx) \\ &= g_2 x &&s\in\mrm{Stab}_G(x) \\ &= \phi(g_2 S) \end{align*}$$ so $\phi$ is well-defined.

Now we check that $\phi$ is surjective. For any $gx\in Gx$, we trivially have $\phi(gS) = gx$.

Next we check that $\phi$ is injective. Suppose $$ \phi(g_1 S) = \phi(g_2 S), $$ then $$\begin{align*} g_1 x &= g_2 x \\ g_2^{-1}g_1 x &= g_2^{-1} g_2 x \\ (g_2^{-1}g_1)x &= x \end{align*}$$ so $$ g_2^{-1} g_1 \in S. $$ Therefore $$ g_1 = g_2(g_2^{-1} g_1) \in g_2 S. $$ Since left cosets partition and $g_1 S$ and $g_2 S$ contain the same element $g_1$, $g_1 S = g_2 S$ as required.
