Let $$ \mu(z) = \frac{az+b}{cz+d}. $$ If $c=0$, then $\mu(\infty) = \infty$ gives a fixed point.

If $c\neq 0$, a fixed point $w$ satisfies the equation $$ w = \frac{aw+b}{cw+d}, $$ which is equivalent to $$ cw^2 + (d-a)w - b = 0.$$ This is a quadratic equation as $c\neq 0$, so there must always be a solution for $w$.
