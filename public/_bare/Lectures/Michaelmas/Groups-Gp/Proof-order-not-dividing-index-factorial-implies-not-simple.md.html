Consider the homomorphism associated with the action $G\actson G/H$, $$\begin{align*} \phi: G &\to \mrm{Sym}(G/H) \\ g &\mapsto (\gamma H \mapsto g\gamma H). \end{align*}$$

For the kernel, if $\phi(g) = \mrm{Id}$, then $gH = H$ so $g\in H$. Hence $\ker\phi \leq H$.

Also $\im(\phi) \leq \mrm{Sym}(G/H)$ so $|\im(\phi)| \ \big| \ |G/H|!$.

Now the isomorphism theorem implies that $$ |G| = |\ker\phi||\im\phi|\ \big|\ |\ker\phi||G/H|! $$ and since $|G| \nmid |G/H|!$, $\ker\phi$ is not trivial.

Hence $\ker\phi$ is a non-trivial normal subgroup of $G$ inside $H$.
