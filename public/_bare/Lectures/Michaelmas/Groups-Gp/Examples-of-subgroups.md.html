• Every group $G$ is a subgroup of itself: $G\leq G$.

  • For every group $G$, $1_G = \{ e_G \}\leq G$ is called the trivial subgroup.

Other groups are called proper subgroups.

  • $\mathbb Z\leq\mathbb Q\leq\mathbb R\leq\mathbb C$.

Proposition (subgroups of integers) open_in_new

The subgroups of $\mathbb Z$ are exactly the subsets $$ n\mathbb Z = \{nk\in \mathbb Z: k\in\mathbb Z\} $$ for all $n=0,1,2,\ldots$.
