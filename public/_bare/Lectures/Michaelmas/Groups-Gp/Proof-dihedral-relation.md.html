We will check the action of the two maps $sr$ and $r^{-1} s$ on three points $$ x = 1,\quad y = e^{2\pi i/n},\quad z = e^{-2\pi i/n}. $$

We have $$\begin{align*} sr(x) = s(e^{2\pi i/n}) &= e^{-2\pi i/n} = r^{-1}(1) = r^{-1}s(x) \\ sr(y) = s(e^{2\pi i(2)/n}) &= e^{-4\pi i/n} = r^{-1}(e^{-2\pi i/n}) = r^{-1}s(y) \\ sr(z) = s(1) &= 1 = r^{-1}(e^{2\pi i/n}) = r^{-1}s(z), \end{align*}$$

so $sr(r^{-1} s)^{-1}$ fixes $x,y,z$. By the three-point lemma, $sr(r^{-1} s)^{-1} = \mathrm{Id}_X$, so $$ sr = r^{-1} s.$$
