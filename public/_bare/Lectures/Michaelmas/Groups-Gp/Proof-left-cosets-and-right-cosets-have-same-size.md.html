Define a mapping $$\begin{align*} f: G / H &\to H\backslash G \\ \gamma H &\mapsto H\gamma^{-1}. \end{align*}$$

This is well-defined because if $\gamma_1 H = \gamma_2 H$ for some $\gamma_1,\gamma_2\in G$, $$\begin{align*} && g&\in H\gamma_1^{-1} \\ &\iff & g &= h_1\gamma_1^{-1} &&h_1\in H \\ &\iff & g^{-1} &= \gamma_1h_1^{-1} \\ &\iff & g^{-1} &\in\gamma_1H \\ &\iff & g^{-1} &\in \gamma_2 H \\ &\iff & g^{-1} &=\gamma_2 h_2 &&h_2\in H \\ &\iff & g &= h_2^{-1} \gamma_2^{-1} \\ &\iff & g &\in H\gamma_2^{-1}, \end{align*}$$ so $H\gamma_1^{-1} = H\gamma_2^{-1}$.

Define another mapping $$\begin{align*} f^{-1}: H\backslash G &\to G / H \\ H\gamma &\mapsto \gamma^{-1}H. \end{align*}$$ This is well-defined for a similar reason: if $H\gamma_1 = H\gamma_2$ for some $\gamma_1,\gamma_2\in G$, $$\begin{align*} && g&\in \gamma_1^{-1}H \\ &\iff & g &= \gamma_1^{-1}h_1 &&h_1\in H \\ &\iff & g^{-1} &= h_1^{-1}\gamma_1 \\ &\iff & g^{-1} &\in H\gamma_1 \\ &\iff & g^{-1} &\in H\gamma_2 \\ &\iff & g^{-1} &=h_2 \gamma_2 &&h_2\in H \\ &\iff & g &= \gamma_2^{-1} h_2^{-1} \\ &\iff & g &\in \gamma_2^{-1}H, \end{align*}$$ so $\gamma_1^{-1}H = \gamma_2^{-1}H$.

$f$ and $f^{-1}$ are inverses since for all $\gamma\in G$, $$ f^{-1}\big(f(\gamma H)\big) = f^{-1}(H\gamma^{-1}) = (\gamma^{-1})^{-1} H = \gamma H. $$

Hence $f$ is a bijection between $G/H$ and $H\backslash G$. Therefore $$ |G/H| = |H\backslash G|. $$
