First, we will show that every $x\in X$ is in at least one orbit. This is easy since $$ x = e\cdot x \in Gx. $$

Next, we will show that if $x\in Gy$ and $x\in Gz$ for some $y,z\in X$, then $Gy=Gz$. Let $$ gy = x = hz $$ for some $g,h\in G$.

To show that $Gy\subseteq Gz$, by $gy = hz$, $$\begin{align*} g^{-1}(gy) &= g^{-1}(hz) \\ y &= (g^{-1}h)z. \end{align*}$$ Therefore for all $ky\in Gy$, $$\begin{align*} ky &= k((g^{-1}h)z) \\ &= (kg^{-1}h)z \in Gz.\\ \end{align*}$$

Swapping the roles for $y,z$, we see that $Gz\subseteq Gy$ equally, so $Gy = Gz$ as required.
