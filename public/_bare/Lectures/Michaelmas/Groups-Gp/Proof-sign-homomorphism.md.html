To see that $\sign$ is well-defined, suppose $\tau_1,\ldots,\tau_k$ and $\tau'_1,\ldots,\tau'_l$ are transpositions such that $$ \tau_1\tau_2\ldots\tau_k = \tau'_1\tau'_2\ldots\tau'_l. $$ Then by $\tau_i\inv = \tau_i$, $$ \tau_k\tau_{k-1}\ldots\tau_2\tau_1\ \tau'_1\tau'_2\ldots\tau'_l = e $$ so by the previous lemma, $$\begin{align*} k+l&\equiv 0\mod 2\\ k&\equiv l\mod 2 \\ (-1)^k&=(-1)^l \end{align*}$$ so $\sign(\tau_1\ldots\tau_k) = \sign(\tau'_1\ldots\tau'_l)$ as required.

To see that $\sign$ is a homomorphism, note that $$\begin{align*} \sign(\tau_1\ldots\tau_k \tau'_1\ldots\tau'_l) &= (-1)^{k+l} \\ &= (-1)^k (-1)^l \\ &= \sign(\tau_1\ldots\tau_k)\sign(\tau'_1\ldots\tau'_l). \end{align*}$$
