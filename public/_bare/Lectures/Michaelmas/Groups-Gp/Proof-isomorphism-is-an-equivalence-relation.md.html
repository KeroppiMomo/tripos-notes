We will check all three conditions of a equivalence relation:

  • Reflexivity: the identity map $\mathrm{Id}_G: G\to G$ is a bijection and a homomorphism, so $G\cong G$.

  • Symmetry: if $G\cong H$, then there exists an isomorphism $\phi: G\to H$. And by this, $\phi^{-1}: H\to G$ is also an isomorphism. Therefore $H\cong G$.

  • Transitivity: suppose $G\xrightarrow{\phi} H\xrightarrow{\psi} K$ are isomorphisms. The composition of homomorphisms is a homomorphism, and the composition of bijections is a bijection, so $\psi\circ\phi: G\to K$ is a homomorphism. Therefore $G\cong K$.
