A $k$-cycle is even iff $k$ is odd since $$ \pmat{a_1&a_2&\ldots&a_k} = \underbrace{\pmat{a_1&a_2}\pmat{a_2&a_3}\ldots\pmat{a_{k-2}&a_{k-1}}\pmat{a_{k-1}&a_k}}_{k-1\text{ transpositions}}. $$

So a $(k_1,k_2,\ldots,k_l)$-cycle is even iff there are an even number of odd cycles, i.e. the number of even $k_i$ is even.
