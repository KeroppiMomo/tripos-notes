We will use the subgroup criterion.

  1. Firstly, $\phi(e_G) = e_H \in \mathrm{im}\,\phi$ so $\mathrm{im}\,\phi\neq\emptyset$. Also for $h_1=\phi(g_1), h_2=\phi(g_2)\in\mathrm{im}\,\phi$, $$\begin{align*} h_1 h_2^{-1} &= \phi(g_1)\phi(g_2)^{-1} \\ &= \phi(g_1)\phi(g_2^{-1}) \\ &= \phi(g_1g_2^{-1}) \in \mathrm{im}\,\phi. \\ \end{align*}$$

  2. First, $\phi(e_G) = e_H$ so $e_G \in \mathrm{ker}\,\phi$ and $\mathrm{ker}\,\phi\neq\emptyset$. Furthermore for $g_1,g_2\in\mathrm{ker}\,\phi$, $$\begin{align*} \phi(g_1 g_2^{-1}) &= \phi(g_1)\phi(g_2^{-1}) \\ &= \phi(g_1)\phi(g_2)^{-1} \\ &= e_H\cdot e_H^{-1} \\ &= e_H \end{align*}$$ so $g_1g_2^{-1}\in\mathrm{ker}\, \phi$.
