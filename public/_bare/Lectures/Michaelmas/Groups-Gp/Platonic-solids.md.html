Definition (Platonic solid) open_in_new

A convex polyhedra $X\subseteq \R^3$ is a Platonic solid if

  • every face is a regular $n$-gon for some $n$;

  • $G=\mrm{Isom}(X)$ acts transitively on the faces;

  • if $x\in X$ is the midpoint of a face, then $\mrm{Stab}_G(x)\isom D_{2n}$.

Theorem (all Platonic solids) open_in_new

There are, up to similarity, 5 Platonic solids:

  • the tetrahedron (4 triangular faces, 4 vertices);

  • the cube (hexahedron) (6 square faces, 8 vertices);

  • the octahedron (8 triangular faces, 6 vertices);

  • the dodecahedron (12 pentagonal faces, 20 vertices);

  • the icosahedron (20 triangular faces, 12 vertices).

Dual polyhedra open_in_new

Two solids $x,y$ are dual if $y$ can be constructed by putting vertices in the centres of the faces of $x$ and then joining the vertices in adjacent faces by edges.

For the Platonic solids:

  • the tetrahedron is dual with itself;

  • the cube and the octahedron are dual; and

  • the dodecahedron and the icosahedron are dual.

If $X$ and $Y$ are dual, then $\mrm{Isom}(X)\isom\mrm{Isom}(Y)$.

Lemma (isometry group of tetrahedron) open_in_new

The group of the tetrahedron is isomorphic to $S_4$, and the subgroup of rotational symmetries is isomorphic to $A_4$.

Lemma (isometry group of cube or octahedron) open_in_new

The isometry group of the cube or the octahedron is isomorphic to $S_4\times C_2$ and the subgroup of rotation symmetries is isomorphic to $S_4$.
