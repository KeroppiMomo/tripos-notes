We would like to construct a Mobius transformation such that $$\begin{align*} z_1 &\mapsto 0, \\ z_2 &\mapsto 1, \\ z_3 &\mapsto \infty. \\ \end{align*}$$ The map $$ \alpha(z) = \frac{z-z_1}{z-z_3}\frac{z_2-z_3}{z_2-z_1} $$ satisfies that.

Similarly to have $$\begin{align*} w_1 &\mapsto 0, \\ w_2 &\mapsto 1, \\ w_3 &\mapsto \infty, \\ \end{align*}$$ we define $$ \beta(z) = \frac{z-w_1}{z-w_3}\frac{w_2-w_3}{w_2-w_1} $$

Therefore $\mu = \beta\inv\circ\alpha$ satisfies the requirement.

For $\mu,\nu\in\mathcal M$ that both sends $z_i$ to $w_i$ for all $i=1,2,3$, note that $\nu\circ\mu\inv$ fixes all $z_i$. Hence by the three-point lemma, $\nu\circ\mu\inv = \mrm{Id}$ so $\nu=\mu$. Therefore $\mu$ is unique.
