Definition (orbits and stabilisers) open_in_new

Let $G\curvearrowright X$ and let $x\in X$.

  • The orbit of $x$ is the set $$ Gx = \sb{gx}{g\in G}. $$

  • The stabiliser of $x$ is the set $$ \mathrm{Stab}_G (x) = \sb{g\in G}{gx = x}. $$

Definition (transitive action) open_in_new

$G\actson X$ is called transitive when: (equivalent definitions)

  • $Gx = X$ for all $x\in X$;

  • for all $x,y\in X$, there exists $g\in G$ such that $gx = y$.

Definition (faithful action) open_in_new

$G \actson X$ is faithful when: (equivalent definitions)

  • there does not exist $g\in G\setminus\se{e}$ such that $gx = x$ for all $x\in X$;

  • for all $g\in G\setminus\se{e}$, there exists $x\in X$ where $gx \neq x$;

  • the associated homomorphism $G\to\mrm{Sym}(X)$ is injective.

Proposition (stabilisers form a subgroup) open_in_new

Suppose $G\actson X$. Then for all $x\in X$, $\mrm{Stab}_G(x) \leq G$.

Proposition (orbits partition) open_in_new

Suppose $G\actson X$. The set of all orbits $$ \sb{Gy}{y\in X} $$ form a partition of $X$, i.e. every $x\in X$ is in exactly one orbit.
