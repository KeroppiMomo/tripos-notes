For $$ p(x) y'' + q(x) y' + r(x) y = 0, $$ we seek for a series solution around $x_0$.

Classification of singular points open_in_new

Definition (ordinary and singular point) open_in_new

If $q(x)/p(x)$ and $r(x)/p(x)$ are analytic at $x=x_0$, i.e. have Taylor series around $x=x_0$, then $x=x_0$ is called an ordinary point. Otherwise it is a singular point.

Definition (regular and irregular singular point) open_in_new

Suppose $x=x_0$ is a singular point. If the equation can be written as $$ P(x) (x-x_0)^2 y'' + Q(x) (x-x_0) y' + R(x) y = 0 $$ where $Q(x)/P(x)$ and $R(x)/P(x)$ are analytic at $x=x_0$, then $x=x_0$ is a regular singular point. Otherwise it is an irregular singular point.

Equivalently, $x=x_0$ is a regular singular point iff $$ \frac{(x-x_0) q(x)}{p(x)}\quad\text{and}\quad\frac{(x-x_0)^2 r(x)}{p(x)} $$ are analytic at $x=x_0$.

Method of Frobenius open_in_new

This method applies for solutions about a regular singular point.

The solutions depend on the roots $\sigma_1, \sigma_2$ of the indicial equation:

  • If $\sigma_2 - \sigma_1\not\in \Z$, then we have two linearly independent Frobenius series solution, $$ y_1(x) = (x - x_0)^{\sigma_1} \sumi n0\infty a_n(x-x_0)^n, $$ $$ y_2(x) = (x - x_0)^{\sigma_2} \sumi n0\infty b_n(x-x_0)^n. $$

  • If $\sigma_2 - \sigma_1 \in\Z$, suppose $\sigma_1 \leq \sigma_2$. Then we have one Frobenius series solution $$ y_1(x) = (x-x_0)^{\sigma_2} \sumi n0\infty a_n(x-x_0)^n $$ and another solution involving $\ln$, $$ y_2(x) = (x-x_0)^{\sigma_1} \sumi n0\infty b_n(x-x_0)^n + cy_1(x)\ln(x-x_0). $$ for some constant $c$ determined by $b_0$.
