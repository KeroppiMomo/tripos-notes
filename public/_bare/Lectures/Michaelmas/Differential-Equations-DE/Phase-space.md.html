For an $n$-th order linear ODE $$ y^{(n)} + p(x) y^{(n-1)} + \ldots + q(x) y = f(x), $$ $y^{(n)}(x_0)$ is determined by $y(x_0), y'(x_0), y''(x_0), \ldots, y^{(n-1)}(x_0)$. Similarly by differentiating the ODE, we can determine higher derivatives.

Therefore we can construct a Taylor series of $y(x)$ about $x_0$ if $y(x_0), y'(x_0), y''(x_0), \ldots, y^{(n-1)}(x_0)$ are specified. By some theorem, a solution $y(x)$ is determined by these initial conditions.

The state of system is fully specified at any $x$ by the solution vector $$ \vec Y(x) = \begin{pmatrix} y(x) \\ y'(x) \\ y''(x) \\ \vdots \\ y^{(n-1)}(x) \end{pmatrix}. $$ This means at any $x=x_0$, $\vec Y(x_0)$, a point in the $n$ dimensional phase space, determines the full solution $y(x)$.

Definition (Wronskian) open_in_new

The Wronskian of $n$ functions $y_i(x)$ for $i=1,2,\ldots, n$ is the determinant of the fundamental matrix, defined by $$ W(x) = \begin{vmatrix} y_1 & y_2 & \ldots & y_n \\ y_1' & y_2' & \ldots & y_n' \\ y_1'' & y_2'' & \ldots & y_n'' \\ \vdots & \vdots & & \vdots \\ y_1^{(n-1)} & y_2^{(n-1)} & \ldots & y_n^{(n-1)} \end{vmatrix}. $$

Proposition (linear dependence and zero Wronskian) open_in_new

If $n$ functions $\{y_i(x)\}$ are linearly dependent, then their Wronskian $W(x) = 0$ for all $x$.

Its contraposition is that if $W(x) \neq 0$, then $\{y_i(x)\}$ are linearly independent.

Proof

Theorem (Abel's theorem) open_in_new

Given any two solutions of a linear homogenous second-order ODE $$ y'' + p(x) y' + q(x) y = 0, $$ if $p(x),q(x)$ are continuous on an interval $I$, then Abel's identity states that the Wronskian satisfies Abel's identity $$ W(x) = W(x_0) \exp\Big(-\int_{x_0}^x p(u) \,du\Big). $$

In particular either

  • $W(x) = 0$ for all $x\in I$; or

  • $W(x) \neq 0$ for all $x\in I$.

Proof

This tells us that two solution vectors $\vec Y_1(x), \vec Y_2(x)$ are either always collinear in the phase space or never as $x$ varies. Therefore, if $\vec Y_1(x_0), \vec Y_2(x_0)$ form a basis, then $\vec Y_1(x), \vec Y_2(x)$ also form a basis at any $x$.

Corollary (Wronskian and missing y' term) open_in_new

For a homogenous linear second-order ODE with missing $y'$ term $$ y'' + q(x) y = 0, $$ the Wronskian $W(x)$ is a constant function.

Proof

Reduction of order using Abel's theorem open_in_new

By Abel's identity, $$ y_1 y_2' - y_1' y_2 = W(x_0) \exp\Big(-\int_{x_0}^x p(u)\,du\Big), $$ which, if $y_1$ is known, gives a first-order linear ODE of $y_2$. Using quotient rule, $$ \frac{d}{dx}\frac{y_2}{y_1} = \frac{W(x_0)}{y_1^2}\exp\Big(-\int_{x_0}^x p(u)\,du\Big) $$ $$ y_2(x) = y_1(x) \int^x \frac{\exp\Big(-\int^\xi p(u)\,du\Big)}{y_1(\xi)^2}\,d\xi, $$ which is equivalent to the reduction of order formula.
