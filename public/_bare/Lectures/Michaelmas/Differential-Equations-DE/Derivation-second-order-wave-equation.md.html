Since partial derivatives commute, we can write the PDE as $$ \Big(\pdd t - c\pdd x\Big)\Big(\pdd t + c\pdd x\Big)\psi = 0. $$

Let $\xi = x + ct, \eta = x - ct$. Note that at constant $\xi$ or constant $\eta$, $\partial\psi/\partial t - c\partial\psi/\partial x$ or $\partial\psi/\partial t + c\partial\psi/\partial x$ vanishes respectively. Therefore it makes sense to change the independent variables to $\xi$ and $\eta$.

Using chain rule, $$\begin{align*} \pdd x &= \pdfd \xi x \pdd\xi + \pdfd\eta x \pdd\eta \\ &= \pdd\xi + \pdd\eta \\ \pdd t &= \pdfd \xi t \pdd\xi + \pdfd\eta t \pdd\eta \\ &= c \pdd\xi - c \pdd\eta. \end{align*}$$ So $$\begin{align*} \pdd t - c\pdd x &= 2c\pdd\xi \\ \pdd t + c\pdd x &= -2c\pdd\eta. \end{align*}$$ The PDE becomes $$ -4c^2 \frac{\partial^2\psi}{\partial\xi\partial\eta} = 0 $$ Integrating by $\xi$ then $\eta$ gives $$ \psi = f(\xi) + g(\eta). $$
