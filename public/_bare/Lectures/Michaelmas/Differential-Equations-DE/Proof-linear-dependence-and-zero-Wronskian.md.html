Linear dependence gives $$ \sum_{i=1}^n c_i y_i(x) = 0 \quad\forall x$$ for some constants $c_i$ not all zero.

Differentiating $k$ times shows that $$ \sum_{i=1}^n c_i y_i^{(k)}(x) = 0 \quad\forall x$$ the $k$-th derivatives are linearly dependent for any $k\in\N$.

Therefore, the solution vectors are also linearly dependent, $$ \sum_{i=1}^n c_i \vec Y_i(x) = \sum_{i=1}^n c_i\begin{pmatrix} y_i(x) \\ y_i'(x) \\ y_i''(x) \\ \vdots \\ y_i^{(n-1)}(x) \end{pmatrix} = 0 \quad\forall x. $$

Since the columns of the fundamental matrix are the solution vectors $\begin{pmatrix}\vec Y_1, \vec Y_2, \ldots, \vec Y_n\end{pmatrix}$, the linear dependence gives that the determinant is 0, so $$ W(x) = 0 \quad\forall x. $$
