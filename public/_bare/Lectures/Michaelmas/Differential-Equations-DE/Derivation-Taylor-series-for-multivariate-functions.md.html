Let $\delta\v x = s\vhat s = (\delta x, \delta y)$. Treating $f$ as a function of $s$, we can perform Taylor expansion: $$\begin{align*} f(\v x_0 + \delta\v x) &= f(\v x_0) + s\dfd fs\Big|_{\v x_0} + \frac12 s^2 \dfd[2] fs\Big|_{\v x_0} + \ldots \\ &= f(\v x_0) + s\vhat s\cdot\vnabla f\big|_{\v x_0} + \frac12s^2 (\vhat s\cdot\vnabla)(\vhat s\cdot \vnabla f)\big|_{\v x_0} + \ldots \\ &= f(\v x_0) + \delta\v x\cdot\vnabla f\big|_{\v x_0} + \frac12\Big(\delta x\pdd x + \delta y\pdd y\Big)\Big(\delta x\pdfd fx + \delta y\pdfd fy\Big) + \ldots \\ &= f(\v x_0) + \delta\v x\cdot\vnabla f\big|_{\v x_0} + \frac12\Big(\delta x^2\pdfd[2] fx + \delta x\delta y\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x\partial y} + \delta x\partial y\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y\partial x} + \delta y^2\pdfd[2] fy\Big)\Big|_{\v x_0} + \ldots \\ &= f(\v x_0) + \delta\v x\cdot\vnabla f\big|_{\v x_0} + \frac12\pmat{\delta x&\delta y}\pmat{f_{xx} & f_{xy} \\ f_{yx} & f_{yy}}\Big|_{\v x_0}\pmat{\delta x\\\delta y} + \ldots \\ &= f(\v x_0) + \delta\v x\cdot\vnabla f\big|_{\v x_0} + \frac12\delta\v x (\vnabla\vnabla f)\big|_{\v x_0} \delta \v x^T + \ldots. \end{align*}$$
