Definition (partial differentiation) open_in_new

Given function $f(x,y)$, the partial derivative of $f$ with respect to $x$ is $$ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\Big|_y = \lim_{\delta x\to 0} \frac{f(x+\delta x, y) - f(x,y)}{\delta x} $$ and that with respect to $y$ is $$ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\Big|_x = \lim_{\delta y\to 0} \frac{f(x, y+\delta y) - f(x,y)}{\delta y}. $$

Notation (partial differentiation) open_in_new

We usually omit the evaluation line and write $$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\Big|_y \equiv \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \equiv f_x.$$

Mixed partial differentiation is written as $$\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\Big(\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\Big) \equiv \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x\partial y} \equiv f_{yx}.$$

Theorem (Schwarz theorem) open_in_new

If $\partial^2 f/\partial x\partial y$ is continuous, then $$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x\partial y} = \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y\partial x}.$$

Theorem (multi-variate chain rule) open_in_new

For a function $f(x,y)$ where $x$ and $y$ are functions of $t$, $$ \frac{df}{dt} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\frac{dx}{dt} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\frac{dy}{dt}. $$

Integrals of a multi-variate function open_in_new

For a function $f(x,y)$, $$ \Delta f = \int df = \int\Big(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}dx + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}dy\Big) $$

This does not depend on the path, only on the start and end points.

Implicit differentiation open_in_new

Define $$ \frac{\partial y}{\partial x}\Big|_z $$ be the partial derivative of $y$ with respect to $x$, holding $z$ fixed. This means that $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\Big|_z = 0. $$

Proposition (partial derivative of implicit equation) open_in_new

For a general implicit equation $f(x,y,z) = c$, $$\frac{\partial y}{\partial x}\Big|_z = -\frac{\partial f/\partial x\big|_{y,z}}{\partial f/\partial y\big|_{x,z}}.$$

Theorem (differentiation of an integral with respect to its parameters) open_in_new
