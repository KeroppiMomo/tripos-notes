We will restate the differentiability of $f$ at $a$, $g$ at $b$ as $$\begin{align*} f(x) &= f(a) + (x-a)f'(a) + \epsilon_f(x)\,(x-a) \\ g(y) &= g(b) + (y-b)g'(b) + \epsilon_g(y)\,(y-b) \end{align*}$$ where $\epsilon_f(a) = \epsilon_g(b) = 0$ with $\epsilon_f$ continuous at $a$ and $\epsilon_g$ continuous at $b$.

Then composing, $$\begin{align*} g\big(f(x)\big) &= g(b) + \big(f(x)-b\big)g'(b) + \epsilon_g\big(f(x)\big)\,\big(f(x)-b\big) \\ &= g\big(f(a)\big) + \big((x-a)f'(a) + \epsilon_f(x)\,(x-a)\big)g'\big(f(a)\big) + \epsilon_g\big(f(x)\big)\,\big((x-a)f'(a) +\epsilon_f(x)\,(x-a)\big) \\ &= g(b) + (x-a)\,f'(a)\,g'(b) + (x-a)\ub{\big(\epsilon_f(x)\,g'(f(a)) + \epsilon_g(f(x))\,f'(a) + \epsilon_g(f(x))\,\epsilon_f(x)\big)}_{\sigma(x)} \end{align*}$$

Note that in $\sigma(x)$, as $x\to a$, $\epsilon_f(x)\to0$ and $\epsilon_g(f(x))\to \epsilon_g(b) = 0$ by continuity. Hence $\sigma(x)\to 0$ as $x\to a$.

Informal proof using little o

$$\begin{align*} f'(x) &= \lim_{h\to0}\frac1h\Big(F\big(g(x+h)\big) - F(g(x))\Big) \\ &= \lim_{h\to0}\frac1h\Big(F\big(g(x)+hg'(x) + o(h)\big) - F(g(x))\Big) \\ &= \lim_{h\to0}\frac1h\Big(\big(hg'(x)+o(h)\big)F'\big(g(x)\big) + o\big(hg'(x)+o(h)\big)\Big) \\ &= g'(x)F'\big(g(x)\big) + \lim_{h\to 0}o\Big(g'(x) + \frac{o(h)}{h}\Big) \end{align*}$$

$o(h)/h\to 0$. Also $g'(x)$ is a constant, and the little o of a constant is 0. Therefore the last term vanishes.
