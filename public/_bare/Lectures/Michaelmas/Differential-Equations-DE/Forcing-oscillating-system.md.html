We investigate in the equation $$ m\ddot y + b\dot y + ky = F(t). $$

We usually write it in terms of the dimensionless time $\tau = \omega_0 t$, where $\omega_0 = \sqrt{k/m}$, $$ y'' + 2\kappa y' + y = f(\tau) $$ where $\kappa = b/2m\omega_0$.

For complimentary functions, depending on the value of $\kappa$, the solution has different behaviours:

  • For $\kappa < 1$, light damping/underdamping: $$ y(t) = e^{-\kappa\omega_0 t}(A\sin\omega_{free}t + B\cos\omega_{free}t), $$ where $\omega_{free} = \sqrt{1-\kappa^2}\ \omega_0$.

  • For $\kappa = 1$, critical damping: $$ y(t) = e^{-\kappa\omega_0t}(A + Bt). $$

  • For $\kappa > 1$, heavy damping/overdamping: $$ y(t) = \underbrace{Ae^{-(\kappa - \sqrt{\kappa^2 - 1})\omega_0 t)}}_\text{dominates long-term} + Be^{-(\kappa + \sqrt{\kappa^2 - 1})\omega_0 t}.$$

Derivation of non-forcing oscillating system

For the general solution,

  • in the beginning, the solution is given by both complimentary functions and particular integral, which is called the transient response.

  • But since all complimentary functions eventually decay, the general solution is dominated by the particular integral in the long run. This is the steady-state response.
