Consider the equation $$ a\frac{dy}{dx} + by = 0\quad\text{with } y(0) =y_0.$$

Discretising, the equation becomes $$\begin{align*} \frac ah(y_{n+1} - y_n) + by_n &= 0 \\ y_{n+1} &= \Big(1 - \frac{bh}{a}\Big)y_n. \end{align*}$$

Solving this recurrence relation gives $$ y_n = \Big(1-\frac{bh}{a}\Big)^n y_0. $$

Now setting $h = x_n/n$, $$y_n = \Big(1 - \frac ba\frac{x}n\Big)^n y_0.$$

We expect as $n\to\infty$, this discrete solution tends to the actual solution. Indeed, $$ y = \lim_{n\to\infty} y_n = y_0e^{-(b/a)x}$$ is the exact solution.
