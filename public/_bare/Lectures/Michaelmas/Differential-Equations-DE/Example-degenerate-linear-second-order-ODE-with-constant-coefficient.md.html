Consider the equation $$ y'' - 2\lambda y' + \lambda^2 y = 0. $$

We have degenerate solution $y_1(x) = e^{\lambda x}$.

For the other solution, let $y_2(x) = \nu(x) y_1(x)$. Then we have $$\begin{align*} \nu''y_1 + 2\nu' y_1' + \nu y_1'' - 2\lambda(\nu'y_1 + \nu y_1') + \lambda^2 \nu y_1 &= 0 \\ \nu''y_1 + \nu'(2y_1' - 2\lambda y_1) + \nu\underbrace{(y_1'' -2\lambda y_1' + \lambda^2 y_1)}_0 &= 0 \\ \frac{\nu''}{\nu'} &= -2\frac{y_1'}{y_1} + 2\lambda \\ \ln \nu' &= -2\ln y_1 + 2\lambda x + C \\ &= -2\lambda x + 2\lambda x + C \\ &= C \\ \nu' &= A \\ \nu &= Ax + B \end{align*}$$ Therefore $$ y_2(x) = (Ax + B)e^{\lambda x}. $$

Hence we have linearly independent solutions $e^{\lambda x}$ and $xe^{\lambda x}$.
