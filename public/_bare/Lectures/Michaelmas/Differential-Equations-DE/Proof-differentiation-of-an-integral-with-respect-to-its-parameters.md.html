Notice $$\begin{align*} \frac{dI}{d\lambda} = \lim_{\delta\lambda\to0}\frac{1}{\delta\lambda}\Big(&\int_{a(\lambda+\delta\lambda)}^{b(\lambda+\delta\lambda)} f(x;\lambda+\delta\lambda)\,dx - \int_{a(\lambda)}^{b(\lambda)} f(x;\lambda)\,dx\Big) \\ = \lim_{\delta\lambda\to0}\frac{1}{\delta\lambda}\Big(&\int_{a(\lambda)}^{b(\lambda)} f(x;\lambda+\delta\lambda) - f(x;\lambda)\,dx \\ &+ \int_{b(\lambda)}^{b(\lambda+\delta\lambda)}f(x;\lambda +\delta\lambda)\,dx \\ &- \int_{a(\lambda)}^{a(\lambda+\delta\lambda)}f(x;\lambda+\delta\lambda)\,dx \Big) \end{align*}$$

$$\begin{align*} \text{First term} &= \int_{a(\lambda)}^{b(\lambda)}\lim_{\delta\lambda\to0}\frac{f(x;\lambda+\delta\lambda)-f(x;\lambda)}{\delta\lambda}\,dx \\ &= \int_{a(\lambda)}^{b(\lambda)}\frac{\partial f}{\partial\lambda}(x,\lambda)\,dx \\ \text{Second term} &= \lim_{\delta\lambda\to0}\frac1{\delta\lambda} f(\bar x;\lambda+\delta\lambda)\cdot\big(b(\lambda+\delta\lambda)-b(\lambda)\big) \\ &= f(x;\lambda)\cdot\frac{db}{d\lambda} \end{align*}$$ where $\bar x$ is between $b(\lambda)$ and $b(\lambda+\delta\lambda)$ (inclusively), given by the mean value theorem.

The third term is handled similarly.
