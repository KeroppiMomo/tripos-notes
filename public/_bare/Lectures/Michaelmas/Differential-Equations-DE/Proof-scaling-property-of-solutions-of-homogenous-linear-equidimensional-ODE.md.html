$y(x)$ being a solution means that $$ ax^2 y''(x) + bxy'(x) + cy(x) = 0. $$

Then by $\tilde y(x) = y(\lambda x)$, $$\begin{align*} \tilde y'(x) &= \lambda y'(\lambda x) \\ \tilde y''(x) &= \lambda^2 y''(\lambda x) \end{align*}$$ so $$\begin{align*} ax^2 \tilde y''(x) + bx\tilde y'(x) + c\tilde y(x) &= a(\lambda x)^2 y''(\lambda x) + b(\lambda x)y'(\lambda x) + cy(\lambda x) \\ &= auy''(u) + buy'(u) + cy(u) &u=\lambda x\\ &= 0 \end{align*}$$ as required.
