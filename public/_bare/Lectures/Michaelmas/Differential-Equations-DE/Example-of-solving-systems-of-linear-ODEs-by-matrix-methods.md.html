$$ \dv Y = \pmat{-4 & 24\\ 1 & -2} \v Y + \pmat{4\\1} e^t. $$

Look for $\v Y_c = \v v e^{\lambda t}$: $$\begin{align*} 0 = \det(M-\lambda I) &= \begin{vmatrix} -4-\lambda & 24 \\ 1 & -2-\lambda \end{vmatrix} \\ &= 8 + 6\lambda + \lambda^2 - 24 \\ &= \lambda^2 + 6\lambda - 16 \\ &= (\lambda + 8)(\lambda - 2) \end{align*}$$ so $\lambda = 2, -8$.

We want to find the eigenvectors now. For $\lambda=2$, $$ \pmat{-6 & 24 \\ 1 & -4} \v v = \v 0 \implies \v v = \pmat{4\\1}.$$ For $\lambda=-8$, $$ \pmat{4&24\\1&6} \v v = \v 0 \implies \v v = \pmat{-6\\1}. $$

Therefore, the general complementary function is $$ \v Y_c = A\pmat{4\\1}e^{2t} + B\pmat{-6\\1}e^{-8t}. $$

For the particular solution, we try $$ \v Y_p = \v u e^t. $$ So substituting, $$ \begin{align*} \v u e^t &= M\v u e^t + \pmat{4\\1}e^t \\ (I - M)\v u &= \pmat{4\\1} \\ \pmat{5&-24\\-1&3}\v u &= \pmat{4\\1} \\ \v u &= -\frac19\pmat{3&24\\1&5}\pmat{4\\1} \\ &= -\pmat{4\\1} \\ \end{align*} $$

Hence, $$ \v Y = A\pmat{4\\1}e^{2t} + B\pmat{-6\\1}e^{-8t} - \pmat{4\\1}e^t. $$
