$$ \ub{(\sin x)}_{p(x)}y'' + \ub{(\cos x)}_{q(x)}y' + \ub{2}_{r(x)}y = 0. $$

So $$ \frac{q(x)}{p(x)} = \frac{\cos x}{\sin x}, \qquad \frac{r(x)}{p(x)} = \frac{2}{\sin x} $$ which are non-analytic when $\sin x=0$, i.e. $x=n\pi$.

To investigate whether the singular points are regular, let $x=n\pi + \epsilon$. $$ (x-n\pi)\frac{q(x)}{p(x)} = \frac{\epsilon\cos(n\pi + \epsilon)}{\sin(n\pi + \epsilon)} = \frac{\epsilon}{\sin\epsilon}\cos\epsilon, $$ which is analytic. Also $$ (x-n\pi)^2\frac{r(x)}{p(x)} = \frac{2\epsilon^2}{\sin(n\pi + \epsilon)} = \frac{2\epsilon}{(-1)^n}\frac{\epsilon}{\sin\epsilon} $$ which is also analytic. So $x=n\pi$ is a regular singular point.
