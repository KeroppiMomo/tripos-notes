Considering the limit of the ratio of two functions $\lim_{x\to x_0} f(x)/g(x)$, if

  • $f(x_0)=g(x_0)=0$ or $\pm\infty$;

  • $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ are differentiable at an open interval $I$ around $x_0$ possibly except $x_0$;

  • $g'(x)\neq 0$ for all $x\in I$ but $x\neq x_0$; and

  • $\lim_{x\to x_0} f'(x)/g'(x)$ exists,

then $$ \lim_{x\to x_0} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x\to x_0}\frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}. $$
