Use the substitution $$ z = \ln x. $$ Then $$\begin{align*} \frac{dx}{dz} &= e^z = x \\ \frac{dy}{dz} &= \frac{dx}{dz} \frac{dy}{dx} = x \frac{dy}{dx} \\ \frac{d^2 y}{dz^2} &= \frac{dx}{dz} \frac{d}{dx}\Big(x\frac{dy}{dx}\Big) \\ &= x^2 \frac{d^2 y}{dx^2} + x\frac{dy}{dx} \end{align*}$$ so the ODE becomes $$\begin{align*} f(x) &= ax^2 \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + bx\frac{dy}{dx} + cy \\ &= a\Big(\frac{d^2y}{dz^2} - x\frac{dy}{dx}\Big) + bx\frac{dy}{dx} + cy \\ &= a\frac{d^2y}{dz^2} + (b-a)\frac{dy}{dz} + cy, \end{align*}$$ which has constant coefficients.

Therefore the complimentary function is $y_c \propto e^{k z}$ where $k$ satisfies the characteristic equation $$ ak^2 + (b-a)k + c = 0. $$ For distinct roots $k_1, k_2$, the general complimentary function is $$\begin{align*} y_c(x) &= Ae^{k_1 z} + Be^{k_2 z} \\ &= Ax^{k_1} + Bx^{k_2}. \end{align*}$$ For degenerate root $k$, we have $$\begin{align*} y_c(x) &= Ae^{kz} + Bze^{kz} \\ &= Ax^k + Bx^k \ln x \end{align*}$$
