The Wronskian $W$ is given by $$ W = y_1 y_2' - y_1' y_2. $$ We differentiate this equation, giving $$\begin{align*} W' &= (y_1 y_2'' + y_1' y_2') - (y_1' y_2' + y_1'' y_2) \\ &= y_1y_2'' - y_1'' y_2. \end{align*}$$ Using the fact that $y_1,y_2$ are solutions to the ODE, $$\begin{align*} W' &= y_1(-py_2' - qy_2) - y_2(-py_1' - qy_1) \\ &= p(-y_1y_2' + y_1'y_2) \\ &= -pW, \end{align*}$$ which is a linear first-order ODE. $$\begin{align*} \frac{W'}{W} &= -p \\ W(x) &= W(x_0)\ \underbrace{\exp\Big(-\int_{x_0}^x p(u)\,du\Big)}_{\neq 0} \end{align*}$$ Therefore,

  • if $W(x_0)=0$, then $W(x) = 0$ for all $x\in I$; or

  • if $W(x_0)\neq 0$, then $W(x)\neq 0$ for all $x\in I$.
