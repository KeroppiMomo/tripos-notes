We will prove by induction. $n=1$ is the case of the product rule.

Suppose this is true for $n\geq 1$. $$\begin{align*} f^{(n+1)} &= \frac{d}{dx}\sum_{r=0}^n\begin{pmatrix}n\\r\end{pmatrix}u^{(n-r)}v^{(r)} \\ &= \sum_{r=0}^n\begin{pmatrix}n\\r\end{pmatrix}\big(u^{(n-r+1)}v^{(r)} + u^{(n-r)}v^{(r+1)}\big) \\ &= \sum_{r=0}^n\begin{pmatrix}n\\r\end{pmatrix}u^{((n+1)-r)}v^{(r)} + \sum_{r=1}^{n+1}\begin{pmatrix}n\\r-1\end{pmatrix} u^{((n+1)-r)}v^{(r)}\big) \\ &= u^{(n+1)} + \sum_{r=1}^n\Big[\begin{pmatrix}n\\r\end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix}n\\r-1\end{pmatrix}\Big]u^{(n+1)-r}v^{(r)} + v^{(n+1)} \\ &= u^{(n+1)} + \sum_{r=1}^n\begin{pmatrix}n+1\\r\end{pmatrix}u^{(n+1)-r}v^{(r)} + v^{(n+1)} \\ &= \sum_{r=0}^{n+1}\begin{pmatrix}n+1\\r\end{pmatrix}u^{(n+1)-r}v^{(r)} \\ \end{align*}$$
