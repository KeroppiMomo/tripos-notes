$$ m\ddot y + b\dot y + ky = 0 $$ When $b=0$, the system oscillates at frequency $\omega_0 = \sqrt{k/m}$. Define dimensionless time $\tau = \omega_0 t$. Then $$ \dot y = \frac{dy}{d\tau}\frac{d\tau}{dt} = \omega_0 y' $$ $$ \dot y = \frac{dy'}{d\tau}\frac{d\tau}{dt} = \omega_0^2 y'' $$ so the equation becomes $$\begin{align*} m\omega_0^2y'' + b\omega_0 y' + \ub{k}_{m\omega_0^2}\ y &= 0 \\ y'' + \frac{b}{m\omega_0}y' + y &= 0 \\ y'' + 2\kappa y' + y &= 0 \\ \end{align*}$$ where $\kappa = b/2m\omega_0$.

Then a solution is $e^{\lambda y}$ where $\lambda$ satsifies the characteristic equation $$ \lambda^2 + 2\kappa \lambda + 1 = 0 $$ so $$ \lambda_1, \lambda_2 = -\kappa \pm \sqrt{\kappa^2 - 1}. $$

  • If $\kappa < 1$, we have $\lambda_1,\lambda_2$ complex $$ \lambda_1,\lambda_2 = -\kappa \pm i\sqrt{1 - \kappa^2} $$ so the solution is $$\begin{align*} y(\tau) &= e^{-\kappa\tau} (A\sin(\sqrt{1-\kappa^2}\ \tau) + B\cos(\sqrt{1-\kappa^2}\ \tau) \\ y(t) &= e^{-\kappa\omega_0 t} (A\sin\omega_{free} t + B\cos\omega_{free} t) \end{align*}$$ where $\omega_{free} = \sqrt{1-\kappa^2}\ \omega_0$.

  • If $\kappa = 1$, we have degenerate roots $\lambda_1,\lambda_2 = -1$. Then the solution is $$\begin{align*} y(\tau) &= e^{-\tau}(A+B\tau) \\ y(t) &= e^{-\omega_0 t}(A+B't). \end{align*}$$

    • If $\kappa > 1$, we have real and negative roots $$\begin{align*} \lambda_1 &= -\kappa + \sqrt{\kappa^2 - 1} \\ \lambda_2 &= -\kappa - \sqrt{\kappa^2 - 1} \\ \end{align*}$$ so the solution is $$\begin{align*} y(\tau) &= Ae^{-(\kappa - \sqrt{\kappa^2 - 1})\tau} + Be^{-(\kappa + \sqrt{\kappa^2 - 1})\tau} \\ y(t) &= \underbrace{Ae^{-(\kappa - \sqrt{\kappa^2 - 1})\omega_0 t)}}_\text{dominates long-term} + Be^{-(\kappa + \sqrt{\kappa^2 - 1})\omega_0 t}. \end{align*}$$
