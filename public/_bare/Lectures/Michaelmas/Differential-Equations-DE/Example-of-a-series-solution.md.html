Consider the equation $$ \alpha\frac{dy}{dx} + \beta y = 0. $$

We have $$ \frac{dy}{dx} = \sum_{n=1}^\infty n a_n x^{n-1} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty (n+1)a_{n+1} x^n. $$

Substituting, $$\begin{align*} 0 &= \alpha\sum_{n=0}^\infty (n+1) a_{n+1} x^n + \beta\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n x^n \\ &= \sum_{n=0}^\infty\Big(\alpha(n+1)a_{n+1} + \beta a_n\Big)x^n. \end{align*}$$ Since each coefficient in the power series of 0 is 0, $$\begin{align*} \alpha(n+1)a_{n+1} + \beta a_n &= 0 \\ a_{n+1} &= -\frac{\beta}{\alpha}\frac{1}{n+1} a_n. \end{align*}$$

Now solving the recurrence relation, $$\begin{align*} a_n &= \Big(-\frac\beta\alpha\Big)\frac 1n a_{n-1} \\ &= \Big(-\frac\beta\alpha\Big)^2 \frac 1{n(n-1)} a_{n-2} \\ &= \Big(-\frac\beta\alpha\Big)^3 \frac 1{n(n-1)(n-2)} a_{n-3} \\ &= \ldots \\ &= \Big(-\frac\beta\alpha\Big)^n \frac 1{n!} a_0. \\ \end{align*}$$

Therefore $$ y(x) = a_0 \sum_{k=0}^\infty\frac{1}{n!}\Big(-\frac\beta\alpha\Big)^nx^n,$$ which is the series expansion of the exact solution $y(x) = A e^{-(\beta/\alpha)x}$.
