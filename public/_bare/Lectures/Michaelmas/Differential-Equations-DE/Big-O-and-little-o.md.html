Definition (big O) open_in_new

$f(x)=O(g(x))$ as $x\to x_0$ if
there exists $M>0$ and
there exists $\delta > 0$ such that
for all $x$ where $0<|x-x_0|<\delta$, $$ |f(x)|\leq M|g(x)|. $$

Extending this, $f(x)=O(g(x))$ as $x\to\infty$ if
there exists $M>0$ and
there exists $X>0$ such that
for all $x > X$, $$ |f(x)|\leq M|g(x)|. $$

Definition (little o) open_in_new

$f(x) = o(g(x))$ as $x\to x_0$ if
for all $\epsilon>0$,
there exists $\delta > 0$, such that
for all $x$ where $0<|x-x_0|<\delta$, $$ |f(x)|\leq \epsilon|g(x)|. $$

Similar definition for $x\to\infty$.

Proposition (limit of ratio and little o) open_in_new

For $g(x)\neq 0$, $f(x) = o(g(x))$ as $x\to x_0$ iff $$ \lim_{x\to x_0} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = 0. $$
