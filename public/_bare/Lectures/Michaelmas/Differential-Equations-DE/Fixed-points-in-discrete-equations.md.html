Consider a first-order discrete equation which is independent of $n$ $$ x_{n+1} = f(x_n). $$

Definition (fixed point of a discrete equation) open_in_new

A fixed point of a first-order discrete equation is a value $x_n$ such that $$ x_{n+1} = x_n $$ i.e. $$ f(x_n) = x_n. $$

Using perturbation analysis, let $x_f$ be a fixed point and we let $$ x_n = x_f + \epsilon_n. $$ Then $$ x_f + \epsilon_{n+1} = f(x_f + \epsilon_n) = \underbrace{f(x_f)}_{x_f\text{ by fixed point}} + \epsilon_n f'(x_f) + O(\epsilon_n)^2. $$ Neglecting higher order terms, $$ \epsilon_{n+1} \approx \epsilon_n f'(x_f). $$

Therefore, a stable point is

  • stable when $|f'(x_f)| < 1$;

  • unstable when $|f'(x_f)| > 1$.
