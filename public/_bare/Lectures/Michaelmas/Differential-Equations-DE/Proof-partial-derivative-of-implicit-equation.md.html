Differentiating both sides of $f(x,y,z)=c$ with respect to $x$ fixing $z$ gives $$\begin{align*} 0 &= \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\Big|_{y,z} \frac{\partial x}{\partial x}\Big|_z +\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\Big|_{x,z} \frac{\partial y}{\partial x}\Big|_z +\frac{\partial f}{\partial z}\Big|_{x,y} \frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\Big|_z \\ &= \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\Big|_{y,z} (1) +\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\Big|_{x,z} \frac{\partial y}{\partial x}\Big|_z +\frac{\partial f}{\partial z}\Big|_{x,y} (0) \\ \frac{\partial y}{\partial x}\Big|_z &= -\frac{\partial f/\partial x\big|_{y,z}}{\partial f/\partial y\big|_{x,z}}. \end{align*}$$

The first line uses the multi-variate chain rule.
