Suppose $B$ is formed by adding a scalar multiple $\lambda$ of column $s$ of $A$ onto column $r$, $$ B_{ij} = \begin{cases} A_{ij}, &j\neq r, \\ A_{ij} + \lambda A_{is}, &j=r. \end{cases}$$

Then $$\begin{align*} \det B &= \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) B_{\sigma(1)1}\ldots B_{\sigma(r-1)(r-1)} B_{\sigma(r) r} B_{\sigma(r+1)(r+1)} \ldots B_{\sigma(n)n} \\ &= \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) A_{\sigma(1)1}\ldots A_{\sigma(r-1)(r-1)} (A_{\sigma(r) r} + \lambda A_{\sigma(r) s}\big) A_{\sigma(r+1)(r+1)} \ldots A_{\sigma(n)n} \\ &= \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma)A_{\sigma(1)1}\ldots A_{\sigma(n)n} + \lambda \sum_{\sigma\in S_n}\epsilon(\sigma)C_{\sigma(1)1} \ldots C_{\sigma(n)n} \\ &= \det A + \lambda\det C \end{align*}$$ where $$ C_{ij} = \begin{cases} A_{ij}, &j\neq r,\\ A_{is}, &j=r, \end{cases} $$ which means that the $s$-th and $r$-th columns of $C$ are identical. Therefore, $\det C = 0$ and so $\det B = \det A$.

Again for adding rows onto rows, use $\det A^T = \det A$.
