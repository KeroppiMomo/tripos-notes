Let $$ \v e_1 = (1,0), \qquad \v e_2 = (0,1), $$ $$ \v e_1' = (1,2), \qquad \v e_2 = (-2, 1). $$

We write $$\begin{cases} \v e_1' &= 1\v e_1 + 2\v e_2 \\ \v e_2' &= -2\v e_1 + 1\v e_2 \end{cases}$$ so the change-of-basis matrix is (recall $\v e_j' = P_{ij} \v e_i$) $$ P = \pmat{ 1 & -2 \\ 2 & 1 }.$$

Therefore any vector $\v u = \pmat{u_1 & u_2}^T$ can be transformed using $$\begin{align*} \v u' = P\inv \v u &= \frac15\pmat{1&2\\-2&1}\pmat{u_1\\u_2} \\ &= \pmat{\frac15(u_1 + 2u_2) \\ \frac15(-2u_1 + u_2)}. \end{align*}$$
