Suppose for some column $c$ in $A$, we multiply each entry by a scalar $\lambda$ to form $B$, $$ B_{ij} = \begin{cases} A_{ij}, &j\neq c, \\ \lambda A_{ij}, &j=c. \end{cases} $$

Then $$\begin{align*} \det B &= \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) B_{\sigma(1) 1} \ldots B_{\sigma(c-1)(c-1)} B_{\sigma(c)c} B_{\sigma(c+1)(c+1)}\ldots B_{\sigma(n)n} \\ &= \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) A_{\sigma(1) 1} \ldots A_{\sigma(c-1)(c-1)} \big(\lambda A_{\sigma(c)c}\big) A_{\sigma(c+1)(c+1)}\ldots A_{\sigma(n)n} \\ &= \lambda \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) A_{\sigma(1)1} \ldots A_{\sigma(n) n} \\ &= \lambda \det A. \end{align*}$$

For row, use $\det B^T = \det B$ and the above.
