Consider $\vec x-\lambda \vec y$ for all $\lambda\in\mathbb R$.

$$\begin{align*} 0&\leq (\vec x-\lambda\vec y)\cdot (\vec x-\lambda\vec y) \\ &= \vec x\cdot\vec x - 2\lambda\vec x\cdot \vec y + \lambda^2\vec y\cdot\vec y \end{align*}$$

Suppose $\vec x,\vec y\neq 0$ (otherwise it's clear). Then this is a quadratic inequality on $\lambda$ which is true for all $\lambda$. Therefore $$0\geq \text{Discriminant} = 4(\vec x\cdot\vec y)^2 - 4(\vec x\cdot\vec x)(\vec y\cdot\vec y) $$ $$(\vec x\cdot \vec y)^2 \leq (\vec x\cdot\vec x)(\vec y\cdot\vec y)$$ hence the result.

Equality occurs iff $|\vec x-\lambda\vec y|^2 = 0$, i.e. $\vec x=\lambda\vec y$.
