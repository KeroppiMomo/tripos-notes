For any $z_1, z_2\in\mathbb C$, let $$ \color{yellow} z_1' = z_1 - z_2 $$ and $$ \color{yellow} z_2' = z_2.$$

Rewriting the conventional form of the triangle inequality, $$\begin{align*} |z_1' + z_2'| &\leq |z_1'| + |z_2'| \\ |z_1| &\leq |z_1 - z_2| + |z_2| \\ |z_1 - z_2| & \geq |z_1| - |z_2|. \end{align*}$$

Similarly by letting $z_1'' = z_2$ and $z_2'' = z_1$, we can show that $$ |z_1 - z_2| = |z_2 - z_1| \geq |z_2| - |z_1|. $$

Since one of $(|z_1| - |z_2|)$ and $(|z_2| - |z_1|)$ is non-positive, we have $$ |z_1 - z_2| \geq \big||z_1| - |z_2|\big| $$ as required.
