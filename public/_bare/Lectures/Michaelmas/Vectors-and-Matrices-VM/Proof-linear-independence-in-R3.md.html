Let $$ \vec 0 = \lambda\vec a + \mu\vec b + \nu\vec c. $$ Taking the dot product with $(\vec b\times\vec c)$, the $\vec b$ and $\vec c$ terms vanish, so $$ 0 = \lambda\vec a\cdot(\vec b\times\vec c). $$ Since $\vec a,\vec b,\vec c$ are not coplanar, $\vec a\cdot(\vec b\times\vec c)\neq 0$, so $\lambda = 0$.

Similarly taking the dot product with $(\vec a\times\vec c)$ gives $\mu = 0$, and that with $(\vec a\times\vec b)$ gives $\nu = 0$.
