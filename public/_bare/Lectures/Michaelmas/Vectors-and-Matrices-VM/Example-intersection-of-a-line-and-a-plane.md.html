Suppose we need to find the point of intersection of a line $(\vec x-\vec a)\times\vec t = \vec 0$ and a plane $\vec x\cdot\vec n = \vec b\cdot\vec n$.

Using vector triple product, $$\begin{align*} \vec 0 &= \big((\vec x-\vec a)\times\vec t)\times \vec n \\ &= (\vec x\times\vec t)\times\vec n - (\vec a\times\vec t)\times\vec n \\ &= (\vec x\cdot\vec n)\vec t - (\vec t\cdot\vec n)\vec x - (\vec a\times \vec t)\times\vec n \\ &= (\vec b\cdot \vec n)\vec t - (\vec t\cdot \vec n)\vec x - (\vec a\times \vec t)\times \vec n \\ (\vec t\cdot\vec n)\vec x &= (\vec b\cdot\vec n)\vec t - (\vec a\times \vec t)\times \vec n \\ \end{align*}$$ If $\vec t\cdot\vec n\neq 0$ (line and plane are not parallel), we have a unique point of intersection $$ \vec x = \frac{(\vec b\cdot\vec n)\vec t - (\vec a\times\vec t)\times\vec n}{\vec t\cdot\vec n}. $$

  • RHS = 0, then the line coincide with the plane, so the solution is the entire line; or
