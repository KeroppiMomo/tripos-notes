• Method 1: using the definition of infinite series

[[FILE NOT FOUND: Uniform convergence]] allows for $$ \sum_{m,n=0}^\infty a_{m,n} = \sum_{r=0}^\infty\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_{r-n,n}. $$

Applying, $$\begin{align*} \exp(z_1)\exp(z_2) &= \sum_{m,n=0}^\infty \frac{z_1^m}{m!}\frac{z_2^n}{n!} \\ &= \sum_{r=0}^\infty\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{z_1^{r-n}z_2^n}{(r-n)!n!} \\ &= \sum_{r=0}^\infty\frac{1}{r!}\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{r!}{(r-n)!n!}z_1^{r-n}z_2^n \\ &= \sum_{r=0}^\infty\frac{(z_1 + z_2)^r}{r!} &&\text{binomial theorem} \\ &= \exp(z_1 + z_2) \end{align*}$$ as required.
