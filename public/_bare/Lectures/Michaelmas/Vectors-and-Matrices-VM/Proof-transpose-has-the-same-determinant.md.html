For any $\sigma\in S_n$, pick $\rho = \sigma\inv$. Then we have $$ \epsilon(\sigma) = \epsilon(\rho). $$ Also $$\begin{align*} A_{\sigma(1) 1} A_{\sigma(2) 2} \ldots A_{\sigma(n) n} &= A_{\sigma(\rho(1)) \rho(1)} A_{\sigma(\rho(2))\rho(2)} \ldots A_{\sigma(\rho(n) \rho(n)} \\ &= A_{1 \rho(1)} A_{2 \rho(2)} \ldots A_{n \rho(n)} \end{align*}$$ since this is just a reordering of factors.

Therefore $$\begin{align*} \det A &= \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) A_{\sigma(1) 1} A_{\sigma(2) 2} \ldots A_{\sigma(n) n} \\ &= \sum_{\rho\in S_n} \epsilon(\rho) A_{1 \rho(1)} A_{2 \rho(2)} \ldots A_{n \rho(n)} \\ &= \sum_{\rho\in S_n} \epsilon(\rho) (A^T)_{\rho(1)1} (A^T)_{\rho(2)2} \ldots (A^T)_{\rho(n)n} \\ &= \det A^T. \end{align*}$$
