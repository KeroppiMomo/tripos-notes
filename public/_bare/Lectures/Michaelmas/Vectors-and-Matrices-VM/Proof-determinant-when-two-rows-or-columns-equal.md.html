Suppose $A$ has identical columns $i,j$ ($i\neq j$), i.e. $$ A_{ki} = A_{kj} \quad \forall k.$$

For each $\sigma\in S_n$, we write $$ \sigma = \rho \begin{pmatrix} i & j \end{pmatrix} $$ by letting $\rho\in S_n$.

Then we have $$ \epsilon(\sigma) = \epsilon(\rho)\epsilon\big(\pmat{i & j}\big) = -\epsilon(\rho).$$

Furthermore, using the construction of $\rho$ and then the fact that the $i$-th and $j$-th columns of $A$ are identical, $$\begin{align*} A_{\sigma(i)i} = A_{\rho(j)i} &= A_{\rho(j)j}, \\ A_{\sigma(j)j} = A_{\rho(i)j} &= A_{\rho(i)i}. \end{align*}$$

Therefore $$\begin{align*} \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) A_{\sigma(1) 1} \ldots A_{\sigma(n) n} &= \det A = \sum_{\rho\in S_n} (-\epsilon(\rho)\big)A_{\rho(1)1}\ldots A_{\rho(n)n} \end{align*}$$ so this is 0.

For two equal rows, use $\det A^T = \det A$.
