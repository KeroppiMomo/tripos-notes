Consider matrix $B$ which is constructed by replacing the $I$-th row of $A$ with its $i$-th row where $i\neq I$, i.e. $$ B_{mn} = \begin{cases} A_{mn}, &m\neq I, \\ A_{in}, &m=I. \end{cases} $$ Then since everything other than row $I$ is the same in both matrices, $$ \Delta_{Ij} \text{ of } B = \Delta_{Ij} \text{ of } A\quad \forall j. $$ But $\det B=0$ since two rows of $B$ are identical. Therefore by Laplace expansion, $$ 0 = \det B = \sum_{k=1}^n B_{Ik} \Delta_{Ik} = \sum_{k=1}^n A_{ik} \Delta_{Ik}. $$
