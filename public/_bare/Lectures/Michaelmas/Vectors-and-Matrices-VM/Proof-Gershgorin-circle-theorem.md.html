Consider an eigenvector $\v v\neq 0$ and its eigenvalue $\lambda$, i.e. $$ A\v v = \lambda\v v. $$

This means $$\begin{align*} \sum_{j=1}^n A_{kj} v_j &= \lambda v_k \\ \sum_{j\neq k} A_{kj} v_j &= (\lambda -A_{kk})v_k \end{align*}$$ for any $k = 1,2,\ldots, n$.

Now pick $k$ such that $|v_k|$ is maximum. Then $$\begin{align*} |\lambda - A_{kk}| &= \Big|\sum_{j\neq k} A_{kj} \frac{v_j}{v_k}\Big| \\ &\leq \sum_{j\neq k} \Big|A_{kj} \frac{v_j}{v_k} \Big| &&\text{triangle inequality} \\ &\leq \sum_{j\neq k} |A_{kj}| &&\text{$|v_k|$ is maximum} \\ &=R_k. \end{align*}$$ So $\lambda$ lies within $D_k(A_{kk}, R_k)$.
