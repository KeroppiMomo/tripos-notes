Proof for Hermitian matrix (done in lectures)

Suppose $\lambda_i\neq \lambda_j$ be distinct eigenvalues of Hermitian matrix $H$ with corresponding eigenvectors $\v v_i, \v v_j$. This means $$ H\v v_i = \lambda_i\v v_i, \qquad H\v v_j = \lambda_j\v v_j. $$

Premultiplying the first equation with $\v v_j^\dagger$ gives $$ \v v_j^\dagger H\v v_i = \lambda_i\v v_j^\dagger\v v_i. \tag{1} $$ Now premultiply the second equation with $\v v_i^\dagger$ then take the Hermitian conjugate. Then $$ \v v_j^\dagger H^\dagger\v v_i = \lambda_j^*\v v_j^\dagger\v v_i. $$ Eigenvalues of Hermitian matrices are real, so $\lambda_j^* = \lambda_j$. Also since $H$ is Hermitian, the left hand side matches that of (1). Hence $$ \lambda_i\v v_j^\dagger\v v_i = \lambda_j\v v_j^\dagger\v v_i. $$ Since $\lambda_i\neq\lambda_j$, $$ 0 = \v v_j^\dagger\v v_i = \langle \v v_j | \v v_i\rangle $$ so $\v v_i$ and $\v v_j$ are orthogonal.

Proof for a general normal matrix

Suppose $\lambda_i\neq\lambda_j$ be distinct eigenvalues of normal matrix $N$ with corresponding eigenvectors $\v v_i, \v v_j$. This means $$ N\v v_i = \lambda_i\v v_i, \qquad N\v v_j = \lambda_j\v v_j. $$ Using $NN^\dagger = N^\dagger N$, $$\begin{align*} \v0 &= (N-\lambda_iI)\v v_i \\ 0 &= |(N-\lambda_iI)\v v_i|^2 \\ &= \v v_i^\dagger (N-\lambda_i I)^\dagger (N-\lambda_iI)\v v_i \\ &= \v v_i^\dagger(N^\dagger N - \lambda_iN - \lambda_iN^\dagger + \lambda_i^2)\v v_i \\ &= \v v_i^\dagger(NN^\dagger - \lambda_i^* N - \lambda_i N^\dagger + \lambda_i^* \lambda_i) \v v_i \\ &= \v v_i^\dagger(N-\lambda_i^* I)(N^\dagger - \lambda_i I)\v v_i \\ &= \|(N^\dagger - \lambda_i^* I)\v v_i\| \\ N^\dagger \v v_i &= \lambda_i^* \v v_i \end{align*}$$ and similarly $$ N^\dagger\v v_j = \lambda_j^* v_j. $$

Now $$ \v v_i^\dagger N\v v_j = \v v_i^\dagger (N\v v_j) = \lambda_j \v v_i^\dagger \v v_j$$ but also $$ \v v_i^\dagger N\v v_j = (N^\dagger \v v_i)^\dagger\v v_j = (\lambda_i^*\v v_i)^\dagger \v v_j = \lambda_i\v v_i^\dagger \v v_j$$ so $$ (\lambda_j - \lambda_i)(\v v_i^\dagger \v v_j) = 0. $$

Since $\lambda_j\neq\lambda_i$, $\v v_i^\dagger \v v_j = 0$ and hence $\v v_i$ and $\v v_j$ are orthogonal.
