Note that $$\begin{align*} \langle\v x | \v y\rangle &= \langle M\v x | M\v y\rangle \\ \v x^T \Lambda\v y &= \v x^T M^T\Lambda M\v y \end{align*}$$ for all $\v x, \v y$. Therefore $$ \Lambda = M^T\Lambda M. $$

Since $\langle M(1,0)^T | M(0, 1)^T\rangle = \langle (1,0)^T | (0, 1)^T\rangle = 0$, let $$ M = \pmat{a & \pm b \\ b & \pm a}. $$

Now since $\langle M(1,0)^T | M(1,0)^T \rangle = \langle(1,0)^T | (1,0)^T\rangle = 1$, $$ a^2 - b^2 = 1. $$ Also by hypothesis, $a>0$, so let $$ a=\cosh\alpha, \qquad b=\sinh\alpha $$ for some $\alpha\in\R$. Substituting yields the result.
