Suppose $\lambda$ is an eigenvalue of $H$ with corresponding eigenvector $\v v\neq\v 0$. This means that $$ H\v v = \lambda\v v. $$ Premultiplying by $\v v^\dagger$, $$ \v v^\dagger H \v v = \lambda \v v^\dagger \v v. \tag{*}$$

Taking the Hermitian conjugate of (\*), $$ \v v^\dagger H^\dagger\v v = \lambda^* \v v^\dagger \v v$$ and since $H$ is Hermitian, the left hand side matches that of (\*). So $$ \lambda \v v^\dagger \v v = \lambda^* \v v^\dagger \v v. $$ As $\v v\neq 0$, $\langle\v v|\v v\rangle\neq 0$, so $\lambda = \lambda^*$ and hence $\lambda\in\R$.
