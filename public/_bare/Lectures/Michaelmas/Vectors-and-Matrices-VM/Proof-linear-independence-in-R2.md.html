Let $$ \vec 0 = \lambda\vec a + \mu\vec b.$$ Taking the cross product with $\vec b$, since $\vec b\times\vec b=\vec 0$, $$ \vec 0 = \lambda(\vec a\times\vec b).$$ And as $\vec a\times\vec b\neq 0$, $\lambda = 0$. Similarly taking the cross product with $\vec a$ gives $\mu = 0$. Therefore, $\vec a$ and $\vec b$ are linearly independent.
