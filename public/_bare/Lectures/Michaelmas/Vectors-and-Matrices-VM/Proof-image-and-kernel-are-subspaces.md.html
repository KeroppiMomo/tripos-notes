We will use the subspace condition. Let $\lambda,\mu$ be arbitrary scalars.

If $\vec x,\vec y\in\mrm{image}(f)$, then there exists $\vec a,\vec b\in U$ such that $f(\vec a) = \vec x$ and $f(\vec b) = \vec x$. Then we have $$\begin{align*} \lambda\vec x + \mu\vec y &= \lambda f(\vec a) + \mu f(\vec b) \\ &= f(\lambda\vec a + \mu\vec b) &&f \text{ is a linear map} \\ &\in \mrm{image}(f) &&\lambda\vec a+\mu\vec b\in U. \end{align*}$$ Therefore $\mrm{image}(f)$ is a subspace of $V$.

If $\vec x,\vec y\in\mrm{kernel}(f)$, then $$\begin{align*} f(\lambda\vec x + \mu\vec y) &= \lambda f(\vec x) + \mu f(\vec y) &&f \text{ is a linear map} \\ &= \lambda\vec 0 + \mu\vec 0 &&\vec x,\vec y\in\mrm{kernel}(f) \\ &= \vec 0. \end{align*}$$ Therefore $\lambda\vec x+\mu\vec y\in\mrm{kernel}(f)$, so $\mrm{kernel}(f)$ is a subspace of $U$.
