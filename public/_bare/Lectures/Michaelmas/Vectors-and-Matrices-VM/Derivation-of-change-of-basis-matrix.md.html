Since $\set{\v e_1, \ldots, \v e_n}$ is a basis, we can write $\v e_j'$ as $$ \v e_j' = \sum_{i=1}^n P_{ij} \v e_i $$ where $P_{ij}$ are scalars for $i,j=1,2,\ldots,n$.

Similarly we can write $\v e_i$ as $$ \v e_i = \sum_{k=1}^n Q_{ki} \v e_k'. $$

Substitute this into the previous equation, $$\begin{align*} \v e_j' &= \sum_i P_{ij} \Big(\sum_k Q_{ki} \v e_k'\Big) \\ &= \sum_k\Big(\sum_i Q_{ki}P_{ij}\Big) \v e_k'. \end{align*}$$ By linear independence of $\v e_j'$, $$\begin{align*} \sum_i Q_{ki}P_{ij} &= \delta_{jk} \\ QP &= I \\ Q &= P\inv \end{align*}$$ as required.
