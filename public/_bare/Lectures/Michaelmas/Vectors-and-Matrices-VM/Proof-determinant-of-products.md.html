• Claim: for any $\epsilon,\rho\in S_n$ and matrix $M$, $$ \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) \prod_{i=1}^n M_{\sigma(i)\rho(i)} = \epsilon(\rho) \det M. $$

Proof. Let $$ \sigma = \mu\rho $$ by $\mu = \sigma\rho\inv$. Then $$\begin{align*} \prod_{i=1}^n M_{\sigma(i)\rho(i)} &= \prod_{i} M_{\mu\big(\rho(i)\big)\rho(i)} \\ &= \prod_i M_{\mu(i)i} \end{align*}$$ since applying $\rho$ just multiplies the factors in a different order. Also we have $$ \epsilon(\sigma) = \epsilon(\mu)\epsilon(\rho). $$

Therefore, $$\begin{align*} \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) \prod_i M_{\sigma(i)\rho(i)} &= \sum_{\mu\in S_n}\epsilon(\rho)\epsilon(\mu) \prod_i M_{\mu(i)i} \\ &= \epsilon(\rho)\det M \end{align*}$$ as required.

Now, $$\begin{align*} \det AB &= \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) \prod_{i=1}^n (AB)_{\sigma(i)i} \\ &= \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) \prod_{i=1}^n \Big(\sum_{k_i=1}^n A_{\sigma(i)k_i} B_{k_i i}\Big) \\ &= \sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \Big( \epsilon(\sigma) \sum_{k_1,\ldots,k_n=1}^n \prod_{i=1}^n A_{\sigma(i) k_i} B_{k_i i} \Big) \\ &= \sum_{k_1,\ldots,k_n=1}^n \bigg[\Big(\prod_{i=1}^n B_{k_ii}\Big)\Big(\underbrace{\sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) \prod_{i=1}^n A_{\sigma(i) k_i}}_S\Big) \bigg] \end{align*}$$

In $S$, if two of $k_1,\ldots,k_n$ are the same, then $S$ is the determinant of a matrix with equal columns, so $S=0$.

Therefore, we can consider $k_1,\ldots,k_n$ being a permutation of $1,\ldots,n$ only, so let $k_i = \rho(i)$ for all $i$.

Hence $$\begin{align*} \det AB &= \sum_{\rho\in S_n}\bigg[\Big(\prod_{i=1}^n B_{\sigma(i)i}\Big) \Big(\sum_{\sigma\in S_n} \epsilon(\sigma) \prod_{i=1}^n A_{\sigma(i)\rho(i)} \Big)\bigg] \\ &= \sum_{\rho\in S_n}\Big(\prod_{i=1}^n B_{\sigma(i)i}\Big)\epsilon(\rho)\det A \\ &= \det A \det B. \end{align*}$$
