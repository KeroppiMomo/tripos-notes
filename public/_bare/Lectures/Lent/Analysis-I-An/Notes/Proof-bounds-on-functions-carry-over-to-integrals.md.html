Firstly if $f(x) \leq g(x)$ on some interval $x\in I$, then $$ \begin{align*} f(x) \leq g(x) \leq \sup_I g \implies \sup_I f \leq \sup_I g, \end{align*} $$ so $$ \begin{align*} U(f,\mathcal{D}) = \sum_{j=1}^{n} (x_j - x_{j-1}) \sup_{[x_{j-1}, x_j]} f \leq \sum_{j=1}^{n} (x_j - x_{j-1}) \sup_{[x_{j-1}, x_j]} g \leq U(g,D). \end{align*} $$

Therefore for all dissections $\mathcal{D}$, $$ \begin{align*} I^\star(f) \leq U(f,\mathcal{D}) \leq U(g,\mathcal{D}) \implies I^\star(f) \leq I^\star(g) \end{align*} $$ but $f,g$ are integrable so $\int_{a}^{b} f \leq \int_{a}^{b} g$.
