Fix $\epsilon>0$. By continuity of $f$, there exists $\delta>0$ such that if $|t-x| < \delta$ then $|f(t) - f(x)| < \epsilon$. Therefore for $|h| < \delta$, $$ \begin{align*} \left|\frac{F(x+h) - F(x)}{h} - f(x) \right| &= \left|\frac{1}{h}\int_{x}^{x+h} f(t)\, dt - f(x) \right| \\ &\leq \frac{1}{|h|} \int_{x}^{x+h} \big(f(t)-f(x)\big)\, dt \\ &\leq \frac{1}{|h|} \epsilon|h| = \epsilon. \end{align*} $$ Thus $$ \begin{align*} \lim_{h\to 0} \frac{F(x+h) - F(x)}{h} = f(x). \end{align*} $$
