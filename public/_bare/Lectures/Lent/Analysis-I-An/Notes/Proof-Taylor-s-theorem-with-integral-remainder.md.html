$$ \begin{align*} f(h) &= f(0) + \int_{0}^{h} f'(u)\, du &&\text{FTC 2, $f'$ continuous}\\ &= f(0) + \int_{0}^{h} f'(u) \left(-\dd{u}(h-u)\right)\, du \\ &= f(0) - \left[(h-u) f'(u)\right]_0^h + \int_{0}^{h} (h-u)f''(u)\, du &&\text{integration by part, $f'$ continuous} \\ &= f(0) - hf'(0) + \frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{h} f''(u) \left(-\dd{u}(h-u)^2\right)\, du \\ &= \ldots \\ &= f(0) + hf'(0) + \frac{h^2}{2!}f''(0) + \ldots + \frac{h^{n-1}}{(n-1)!}f^{(n-1)}(0) + \ub{\frac{1}{(n-1)!}\int_{0}^{h} (h-u)^{n-1} f^{(n)}(u)\,du}_{R_n} \\ \end{align*} $$ Substituting $u=th$ gives $$ \begin{align*} R_n = \frac{h^n}{(n-1)!} \int_{0}^{1} (1-t)^{n-1} f^{(n)}(th)\, dt. \end{align*} $$
