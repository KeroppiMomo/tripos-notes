For $|a_{n+1}/a_n|\to l$, use the ratio test: $$ \lim_{n\to\infty} \left|\frac{a_{n+1} z^{n+1}}{a_nz^n}\right| = \lim_{n\to\infty}\left|\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\right||z| = l|z|.$$ For $|a_n|^{1/n}\to l$, use the n-th root test: $$ \lim_{n\to\infty} |a_n z^n|^{1/n} = \lim_{n\to\infty} |a_n|^{1/n}|z| = l|z|. $$ So if $l|z|<1$ we have absolute convergence, and if $l|z|>1$ we have divergence.

For $|a_{n+1}/a_n|\to\infty$ and $z\in\C\setminus\set0$, there exists $N$ such that for all $n\geq N$, $|a_{n+1}/a_n| > 1/|z|$, so $$ |a_n z^n| = \left|\frac{a_n}{a_{n-1}}\right|\left|\frac{a_{n-1}}{a_{n-2}}\right|\ldots\left|\frac{a_{N+1}}{a_{N}}\right||a_N||z|^n \geq \left(\frac{1}{|z|}\right)^{n-N} |a_N||z|^n = |a_N||z|^N > 0. $$ Therefore $a_n z^n\not\to 0$ and by the n-th term test, $\sum a_nz^n$ diverges. Hence $R=0$.

For $|a_n|^{1/n}\to\infty$ and $z\in\C\setminus\set0$, there exists $N$ such that for all $n\geq N$, $|a_n|^{1/n}>1/|z|$, so $$ |a_nz^n| = |a_n||z|^n > 1 > 0.$$ Therefore $\sum a_nz^n$ diverges, so $R=0$.
