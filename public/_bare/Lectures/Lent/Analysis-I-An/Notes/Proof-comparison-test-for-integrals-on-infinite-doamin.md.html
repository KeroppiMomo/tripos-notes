Note that $$ \begin{align*} \int_{a}^{R} f \leq \int_{a}^{R} g \leq \int_{a}^{\infty} g, \end{align*} $$ so $\int_{a}^{R} f$ is bounded. Also $R\mapsto \int_{a}^{R} f$ is increasing since $f\geq 0$. Therefore $$ \begin{align*} \int_{a}^{\infty} f \equiv \lim_{R\to \infty} \int_{a}^{R} f = \sup_{R\geq a} \int_{a}^{R} f. \end{align*} $$
