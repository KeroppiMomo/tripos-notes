Set $$ \begin{align*} F(x) = \int_{a}^{x} f(t)\, dt, \qquad h(t) = F(g(t)) \end{align*} $$ where $h$ is well-defined as $g$ takes values in $[a,b]$.

Then $$ \begin{align*} \int_{\alpha}^{\beta} f(g(t))g'(t)\, dt &= \int_{\alpha}^{\beta} F'(g(t)) g(t)\, dt &&\text{FTC 1} \\ &= \int_{\alpha}^{\beta} h'(t)\, dt &&\text{chain rule} \\ &= h(\beta) - h(\alpha) &&\text{FTC 2} \\ &= F(g(\beta)) - F(g(\alpha)) \\ &= F(a) - F(b) \\ &= \int_{a}^{b} f(t)\, dt. \end{align*} $$
