Let $\mathcal{D}$ be any dissection of $[a,b]$. Apply the mean value theorem to $f$ on $[x_{j-1}, x_j]$ gives some $\xi_j \in (x_{j-1}, x_j)$ such that $$ \begin{align*} f'(\xi_j) \, (x_j - x_{j-1}) = f(x_j) - f(x_{j-1}). \end{align*} $$ Therefore we have the telescoping sum $$ \begin{align*} \sum_{j=1}^{n} f'(\xi_j) \,(x_j - x_{j-1}) = f(b) - f(a). \end{align*} $$ But $$ \begin{align*} L(f',\mathcal{D}) = \sum_{j=1}^{n} (x_j - x_{j-1})\inf_{[x_{j-1}, x_j]} f' \leq \sum_{j=1}^{n} (x_j-x_{j-1}) \,f'(\xi_j) \leq \sum_{j=1}^{n} (x_j-x_{j-1}) \sup_{[x_{j-1}, x_j]} f' = U(f',\mathcal{D}). \end{align*} $$ so $$ \begin{align*} I_\star(f') \leq f(b) - f(a) \leq I^\star(f'). \end{align*} $$ But $f'$ is integrable, so $$ \begin{align*} I_\star(f') = I^\star(f') = \int_{a}^{b} f' = f(b) - f(a). \end{align*} $$
