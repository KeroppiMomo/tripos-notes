For any $\epsilon>0$, let $N_1(\epsilon)$ such that $|a_n - a| < \epsilon$ for $n\geq N_1(\epsilon)$ and let $N_2(\epsilon)$ such that $|c_n - a| < \epsilon$ for $n\geq N_2(\epsilon)$.

For $n\geq\max\{N_1(\epsilon), N_2(\epsilon)\}$, $$\begin{align*} |b_n - a| &= |b_n - a_n + a_n - a| \\ &\leq |b_n - a_n| + |a_n - a| \\ &< |c_n - a_n| + \epsilon \\ &= |c_n - a - a_n + a| + \epsilon \\ &\leq |c_n - a| + |a_n -a| + \epsilon \\ &< 3\epsilon \end{align*}$$ as required.
