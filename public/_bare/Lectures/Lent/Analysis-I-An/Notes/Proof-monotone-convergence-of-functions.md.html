$l=\sup_{x\geq a}f(x)$ exists since $f$ is bounded above.

Fix $\epsilon>0$. Then by the definition of supremum, there exists $K$ such that $$ \begin{align*} f(K) \geq l-\epsilon. \end{align*} $$ Then for all $x\geq K$, $$ \begin{align*} l-\epsilon\leq f(K) \leq f(x) \leq l \end{align*} $$ as required.
