Since $f$ is bounded, there exists $K>0$ such that $|f(x)| \leq K$ for all $x\in[a,b]$.

For the trivial dissection $\mathcal{D}_0 = \set{a,b}$, $$ \begin{align*} U(f,\mathcal{D}_0) &= (b-a)\sup_{x\in[a,b]}f(x) \leq K(b-a), \\ L(f,\mathcal{D}_0) &= (b-a)\inf_{x\in[a,b]}f(x) \geq -K(b-a). \end{align*} $$ Since $\mathcal{D}_0 \subseteq \mathcal{D}$ for any dissection $\mathcal{D}$, we have $$ \begin{align*} -K(b-a) \leq L(f,\mathcal{D}_0) \leq L(f,\mathcal{D}) \leq U(f,\mathcal{D}) \leq U(f,\mathcal{D}') \leq K(b-a). \end{align*} $$ Hence $\sb{U(f,\mathcal{D})}{\text{dissection }\mathcal{D}}$ and $\sb{L(f,\mathcal{D})}{\text{dissection }\mathcal{D}}$ are bounded below and above and are non-empty, and so infimum and supremum exist.
