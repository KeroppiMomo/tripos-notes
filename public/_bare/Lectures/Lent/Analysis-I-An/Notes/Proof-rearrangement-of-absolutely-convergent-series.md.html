For any $\epsilon>0$, since $\fsum n1\infty |a_n|$ converges, there exists $N$ such that $$ \fsum n{N+1}\infty |a_n| < \epsilon\quad \text{if }n\geq N. $$

Pick $M$ such that $\sigma\inv(k) < M$ for all $k=1,2,\ldots, N$. Then if $m\geq M$, $$ \fsum n1\infty a_n - \fsum n1m a_{\sigma(n)} = \sum_{n\in K_m} a_n $$ where $K_m = \N\setminus\sb{\sigma(n)}{n=1,2,\ldots,m} \subseteq \set{N+1,N+2,N+3,\ldots}$. Hence $$ \Big|\fsum n1\infty a_n - \fsum n1m a_{\sigma(n)}\Big| \leq \Big|\fsum n{N+1}\infty a_n\Big| \leq \fsum n{N+1}\infty |a_n| < \epsilon$$ so $\fsum n1m a_{\sigma(n)} \to \fsum n1\infty a_n$ as $m\to\infty$ as required.
