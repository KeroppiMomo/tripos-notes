Let $$\begin{align*} f: \C &\to \C \\ z &\mapsto \bar z. \end{align*}$$

$f$ is continuous since if $|z-a|-\epsilon$, then $|f(z)-f(a)| = |\bar z-\bar a| = |z-a| < \epsilon$.

Consider the difference quotient. Suppose $h_n\in\R$ and $h_n\searrow 0$. For $z_n=h_n + a$, $$ \frac{f(z_n)-f(a)}{z_n-a} = \frac{h_n + \bar a - \bar a}{h_n + a - a} = 1 \to 1,$$ but for $z_n = ih_n + a$, $$ \frac{f(z_n)-f(a)}{z_n-a} = \frac{-ih_n + \bar a - \bar a}{ih_n + a - a} = -1 \to -1,$$ so the limit does not exist.
