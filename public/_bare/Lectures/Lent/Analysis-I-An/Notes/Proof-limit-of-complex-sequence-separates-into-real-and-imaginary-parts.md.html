The key fact we will use is for all $w\in\C$, $$\max\set{|\Re w|, |\Im w|} \leq |w| \leq |\Re w|+|\Im w|.$$

Suppose $z_n\to z$. This means that for all $\epsilon>0$, there exists $N$ such that for all $n\geq N$, $|z_n - z| < \epsilon$. Then $|\Re z_n - \Re z| = |\Re(z_n-z)| \leq |z_n - z| < \epsilon$ and also $|\Im z_n - \Im z| < \epsilon$. Hence $\Re z_n \to \Re z$ and $\Im z_n \to \Im z$.

Conversely suppose $\Re z_n \to \Re z$ and $\Im z_n \to \Im z$. This means for all $\epsilon>0$, there exists $N_1,N_2$ such that $|\Re z_n - \Re z|<\epsilon$ if $n\geq N_1$ and $|\Im z_n - \Im z| < \epsilon$ if $n\geq N_2$. So for $n\geq\max\set{N_1, N_2}$, $$ |z_n - z| \leq |\Re(z_n - z)| + |\Im(z_n - z)| < 2\epsilon $$ and hence $z_n\to z$.
