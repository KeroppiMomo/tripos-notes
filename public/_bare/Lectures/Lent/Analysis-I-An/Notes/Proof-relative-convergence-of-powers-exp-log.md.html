For $x>0$, we can apply an estimate of $e^x$ for any $n\in\N$: $$ e^x = \fsum k0\infty \frac{x^k}{k!} > \frac{x^n}{n!}. $$

  1. Pick $n\in\N$ such that $n\geq r+1$. We have $$ 0\leq\frac{x^r}{e^x}\leq \frac{n! x^r}{x^n}\leq \frac{n!}x $$ so $x^r/e^x\to0$ as $x\to\infty$.

  2. Let $y=\log x$. For $x\geq e$, $$ 0\leq \frac{\log x}{x^r} = \frac{y}{e^{ry}}=\left(\frac{y^{1/r}}{e^y}\right)^r \leq \left(\frac{n!}{y}\right)^r \leq \frac{(n!)^r}{y} = \frac{(n!)^r}{\log x}$$ where $n\geq 1/r+1$ using the above estimation. Since $\log x\to\infty$ as $x\to\infty$, we have $(\log x)/x^r \to 0$.

  3. Let $z=1/x$ above. Then we get $$ 0\leq -z^r\log x\leq -\frac{(n!)^r}{\log z}. $$ As $z\to 0^+$, $\log z\to -\infty$, so $z^r\log x\to0$.
