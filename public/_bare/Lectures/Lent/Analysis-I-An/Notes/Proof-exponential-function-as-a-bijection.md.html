Injectivity follows from being strictly increasing.

For surjectivity, suppose $y\in(0,\infty)$. Then since $\exp(x)\to0$ as $x\to-\infty$ and $\exp(x)\to\infty$ as $x\to+\infty$, there exists $a,b\in\R$ such that $$ \exp(a) < y < \exp(b). $$ Applying the intermediate value theorem to $\exp:[a,b]\to\R$ gives an $x\in\R$ such that $\exp(x)=y$.
