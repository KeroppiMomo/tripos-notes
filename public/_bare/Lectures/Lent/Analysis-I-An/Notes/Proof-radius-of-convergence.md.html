Let $$ A = \sb{r\geq0}{\text{$\exists z\in\C$ with $|z|=r$ such that $\fsum n0\infty a_n z^n$ converges}}. $$

Clearly $0\in A$, so $A$ is non-empty. Let $R=\sup A$ if $A$ is bounded, or $\infty$ if unbounded.

For $|z|>R$, $\sum a_n z_n$ diverges from the definition of $A$.

Suppose $|w|<R$. Then there exists some $z$ with $|w| > |z|\in A$ and $\sum a_n z^n$ converges. Then by the previous lemma, $\sum a_n w^n$ converges absolutely.
