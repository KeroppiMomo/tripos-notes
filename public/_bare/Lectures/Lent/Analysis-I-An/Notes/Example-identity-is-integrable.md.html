Consider $$ \begin{align*} f: [0,1] &\to 1 \\ x &\mapsto x. \end{align*} $$

Let $\mathcal{D}_k = \set{0,\frac{1}{k}, \ldots, \frac{k-1}{k}, 1}$ be the uniform dissection.

Then $$ \begin{align*} U(f,\mathcal{D}_k) &= \sum_{j=1}^{k} \left(\frac{j}{k} - \frac{j-1}{k}\right)\sup_{x\in[\frac{j-1}{k}, \frac{j}{k}]} f(x) \\ &= \sum_{j=1}^{k} \frac{1}{k}\frac{j}{k} \\ &= \frac{k(k+1)}{2k^2} = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2k}. \end{align*} $$ Similarly $$ \begin{align*} L(f,\mathcal{D}_k) &= \sum_{j=1}^{k} \left(\frac{j}{k}-\frac{j-1}{k}\right) \inf_{x\in[\frac{j-1}{k}, \frac{j}{k}]} f(x) \\ &= \sum_{j=1}^{k} \frac{1}{k}\frac{j-1}{k} \\ &= \frac{k(k-1)}{2k^2} = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2k}. \end{align*} $$

Therefore $$ \begin{align*} I^\star(f) &= \inf_\mathcal{D} U(f,\mathcal{D}) \leq \inf_{\mathcal{D}_k} U(f,\mathcal{D}_k) = \frac{1}{2} \\ I_\star(f) &= \sup_\mathcal{D} L(f,\mathcal{D}) \geq \sup_{\mathcal{D}_k} L(f,\mathcal{D}_k) = \frac{1}{2} \end{align*} $$ But $I_\star(f) \leq I^\star(f)$, so $I_\star(f) = I^\star(f) = 1/2$. Hence $f$ is integrable and $\int_{0}^{1} x \, dx = 1/2$.
