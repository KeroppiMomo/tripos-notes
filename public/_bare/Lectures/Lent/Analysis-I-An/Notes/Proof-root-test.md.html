If $a>1$, then there exists $N$ such that $a_n^{1/n} > 1$ for $n\geq N$. So $a_n > 1$ and thus $a_n\not\to 0$. Therefore by the $n$-th term test, $\sum a_n$ diverges.

If $a<1$, then pick $r\in (a,1)$. There exists $N$ such that $a_n^{1/n} < r$ for all $n\geq N$. This means $a_n < r^n$. Since $r<1$, the series $\sum r^n$ converges, so by the comparison test, $\sum a_n$ converges.
