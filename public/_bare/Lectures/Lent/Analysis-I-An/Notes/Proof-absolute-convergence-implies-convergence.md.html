We'll use the general principle of convergence.

Let $S_n=\fsum k1n a_k$ and $\bar S_n = \fsum k1n |a_k|$.

Suppose that $n\leq m$. Then using the triangle inequality, $$\begin{align*} |S_m - S_n| = \Big|\fsum k{n+1}m a_k\Big| \leq \fsum k{n+1}m |a_k| = \bar S_m - \bar S_n. \end{align*}$$

$(\bar S_n)$ is convergent and hence Cauchy. So given $\epsilon>0$, there exists $N$ such that for all $m\geq n\geq N$, $|\bar S_m - \bar S_n| < \epsilon$. Hence $|S_m - S_n| < \epsilon$. So $(S_n)$ is also Cauchy and therefore converges.
