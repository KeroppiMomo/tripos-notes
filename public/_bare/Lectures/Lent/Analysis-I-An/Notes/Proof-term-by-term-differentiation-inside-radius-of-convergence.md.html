Convergence of derivative

First we have to show that $$ g(z) = \fsum n1\infty na_n z^{n-1} $$ has a radius of convergence of $R$.

For $0<|z|<R$, pick $r$ such that $|z|<r<R$. Since $\sum a_n r^n$ converges, by the $n$-th term test, $a_n r^n \to 0$ as $n\to\infty$, so there exists $K$ such that $|a_n r^n| \leq K$ for all $n\geq 0$.

Then $$ |na_n z^{n-1}| = \frac{|a_nr^n|}{|z|}n\left|\frac{z}{r}\right|^n \leq \frac{K}{|z|} n\rho^n,\qquad \rho=\left|\frac zr\right|<1$$ but $\sum n\rho^ n$ converges by the ratio test: $$ \left|\frac{(n+1)\rho^{n+1}}{n\rho^n}\right|=\rho\left(1+\frac1n\right) \to \rho < 1 $$ so $\sum na_nz^{n-1}$ converges absolutely by comparison.

If $|z|>R$, suppose $\sum na_n z^{n-1}$ converges. Then pick $w\in\C$ such that $|z|>|w|>R$. This makes $\sum na_n w^{n-1}$ converges absolutely. But for $n\geq 1$, $$ |a_n w^n| \leq \frac{1}{|z|}|na_n z^{n-1}| $$ so $\sum a_n w^n$ converges absolutely by comparison test, which contradicts with $|w|>R$. Hence $\sum na_n z^{n-1}$ diverges.

This completes the proof for $\sum na_n z^{n-1}$ having a radius of convergence of $R$. Applying this result to the series $\sum na_n z^{n-1}$ shows that $\sum n(n-1)a_n z^{n-2}$ also has a radius of convergence of $R$.

Error term tends to zero

We need to show $$ I = \frac{f(z+h)-f(z)-hg(z)}h \to 0\qquad\text{as $h\to0$}.$$ Fix $z$ with $|z|<R$, and assume $|z|+|h|<r<R$ for some $r$. By the above, all terms in $I$ converge absolutely for $|z|<R$, so $$ \begin{align*} |I| &= \left|\frac1h \lim_{N\to\infty} \fsum n0N a_n\big((z+h)^n-z^n-hnz^{n-1}\big)\right| \\ &= \frac1{|h|} \lim_{N\to\infty}\left|\fsum n0N a_n\big((z+h)^n-z^n-hnz^{n-1}\big)\right| &&\text{$|\cdot|$ continuous} \\ &\leq \frac1{|h|} \lim_{N\to\infty}\fsum n0N |a_n|\big|(z+h)^n-z^n-hnz^{n-1}\big| &&\text{triangle inequality}. \end{align*} $$

Now we need an estimate for $\big|(z+h)^n - z^n - hnz^{n-1}\big|$: using binomial theorem, $$\begin{align*} \big|(z+h)^n - z^n - hnz^{n-1}\big| &\leq \fsum k2n \left|\frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!} h^kz^{n-k}\right| &&\text{triangle inequality} \\ &\leq \fsum k2n \frac{n!}{(k-2)!(n-k)!} |h|^k |z|^{n-k} \\ &\leq n(n-1)|h|^2\fsum k0{n-2} \frac{(n-2)!}{k!(n-2-k)!} |h|^k |z|^{n-2-k} \\ &=n(n-1)|h|^2 (|h|+|z|)^{n-2}. \end{align*}$$

Hence $$\begin{align*} |I|&\leq |h|\lim_{N\to\infty}\fsum n0N n(n-1)\, |a_n|\, (|h|+|z|)^{n-2} \\ &\leq |h|\lim_{N\to\infty}\fsum n0N n(n-1)\, |a_n|\, r^{n-2} \\ &\equiv |h| \,A_r \end{align*}$$ where $A_r$ converges since $\sum n(n-1)a_n z^{n-2}$ has a radius of convergence of $R$. Thus $I\to 0$ as $h\to 0$.
