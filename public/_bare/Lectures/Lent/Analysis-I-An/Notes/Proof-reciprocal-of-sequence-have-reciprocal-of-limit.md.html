For any $\epsilon>0$, let $N(\epsilon)$ such that $|a_n - a| < \epsilon$ for $n\geq N(\epsilon)$.

Note that $$\begin{align*} \left|\frac1{a_n} - \frac1a\right| &= \left|\frac{a - a_n}{aa_n}\right| \\ &= \frac{|a_n - a|}{|a||a_n|}. \end{align*}$$

We bound $|a_n|$ by noticing for $n\geq N(|a/2|)$, $|a_n - a| < |a/2|$, so $$ \begin{align*} |a_n| &= |a_n - a + a| \\ &\geq \big||a| - |a_n - a|\big| \\ &= |a| - |a_n - a|\\ &> |a| - \frac{|a|}2 \\ &= \frac{|a|}2 \\ \end{align*} $$ so for $n\geq N(\epsilon)$, $$ \left|\frac1{a_n} - \frac1a\right| \leq \frac\epsilon{|a|^2 / 2}$$ as required.
