(A monotone function is bounded by the values at its endpoints $f(a),f(b)$ so it makes sense to talk about integrability.)

WLOG suppose $f$ is increasing.

Then $$ \begin{align*} \sup_{x\in[x_{j-1}, x_j]} f(x) &= f(x_j) \\ \inf_{x\in[x_{j-1}, x_j]} f(x) &= f(x_{j-1}). \end{align*} $$ Thus for any dissection $\mathcal{D}$, $$ \begin{align*} U(f,\mathcal{D}) - L(f,\mathcal{D}) = \sum_{j=1}^{n} (x_j-x_{j-1})(f(x_j) - x_{j-1}). \end{align*} $$ Choose $\mathcal{D}_n$ to be the uniform dissection $$ \begin{align*} \mathcal{D}_n = \set{a, a+\frac{1}{n}(b-a), a+\frac{2}{n}(b-a), \ldots, a+\frac{n-1}{n}(b-a), b}. \end{align*} $$ Then we get a telescoping sum $$ \begin{align*} U(f,\mathcal{D}) - L(f,\mathcal{D}) &= \frac{b-a}{n}\sum_{j=1}^{n} (f(a+\frac{j}{n}(b-a)) - f(a+\frac{j-1}{n}(b-a))) \\ &= \frac{b-a}{n}(f(b)-f(a)). \end{align*} $$ Taking $n$ sufficiently large, we see that for all $\epsilon>0$, we can find $\mathcal{D}=\mathcal{D}_n$ such that $$ \begin{align*} U(f,\mathcal{D}) - L(f,\mathcal{D}) < \epsilon \end{align*} $$ and therefore by Cauchy criterion, $f$ is integrable.
