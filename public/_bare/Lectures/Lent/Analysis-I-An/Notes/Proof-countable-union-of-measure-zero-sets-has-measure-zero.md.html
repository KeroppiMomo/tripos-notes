Fix $\epsilon$. For each $k$, since $A_k$ has measure zero, pick $I_j^k$ with $j=1,2,3,\ldots$ such that $$ \begin{align*} A_k \subset \bigcup_{j=1}^{\infty} I_j^k \quad \text{ and } \quad \sum_{j=1}^{\infty} |I_j^k| < \epsilon 2^{-k}. \end{align*} $$ Then consider $\set{I_j^k}_{j,k=1}^{\infty}$, which is countable. We have $$ \begin{align*} \bigcup_{k\in\N} A_k \subset \bigcup_{j,k=1}^{\infty} I_j^k \end{align*} $$ and $$ \begin{align*} \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \sum_{j=1}^{\infty} |I_j^k| < \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \epsilon 2^{-k} = \epsilon. \end{align*} $$ Therefore $\bigcup A_k$ has measure zero.
