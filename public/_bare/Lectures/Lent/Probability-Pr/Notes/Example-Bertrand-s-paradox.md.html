Question: draw a random chord $C$ on a circle. What is the probability $p$ that the length of $C$ exceeds the side length $L$ of an inscribed equilateral triangle?

Three ways of picking a random chord give different answers:

  1. Choose two random points $A,B$ on the circumference and join them to get a chord $C_1$. Construct an inscribed equilateral triangle with $A$ as one of the vertex.

    $AB>l$ iff $B$ is between $D$ and $E$, so $p_1=1/3$.

  2. Choose a random point $P$ inside the circle and take $C_2$ be the unique chord for which $P$ is the midpoint.

    Length of $C_2 > l$ iff $OP < 1/2$, so $P$ has to be inside the circle of radius $1/2$. Hence $p_2=1/4$.

  3. Fix a line and choose a random point $G$ as the centre of a circle. The chord $C_3$ is the intersection of the circle and the line. Condition on the existence of $C_3$.

    Then we can model the distance from $G$ to the line as $H \sim U[-R,R]$. Length of $C_3 > L$ iff $|H| \leq R/2$, so $p_3 = 1/2$.
