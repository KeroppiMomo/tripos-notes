Set $Y_n = X_n - \mu$. Then $\expect(Y_n) = 0$ and $S_n/n - \mu = (Y_1+\ldots+Y_n)/n$. Moreover, $$ \begin{align*} \expect(Y_1^4) = \expect(X_1^4) + 4\mu \expect(X_1^3) + 6\mu^2\expect(X_1^2) + 4\mu^3\expect(X_1) + \mu^4 < \infty \end{align*} $$ since $$ \begin{align*} \expect(X_1^k) \leq \expect(\max\set{X_1^4, 1}) \leq \expect(X_1^4) + 1 < \infty,\quad k=1,2,3. \end{align*} $$

It suffices to show that $$ \begin{align*} \expect\left(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left(\frac{S_n}{n}\right)^4\right) < \infty \end{align*} $$ because $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (S_n/n)^4 < \infty \implies S_n/n\to 0 \text{ as } n\to\infty$ so $$ \begin{align*} \prob\left(\frac{S_n}{n} \to 0 \text{ as } n\to\infty\right) \geq \prob\left(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left(\frac{S_n}{n}\right)^4 < \infty\right) = 1. \end{align*} $$

Note that $$ \begin{align*} S_n^4 = (X_1 + \ldots + X_n)^4 = \sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i^4 + \pmat{4 \\ 2}\sum_{1\leq i<j\leq n} X_i^2X_j^2 + R \end{align*} $$ where $R$ is a finite sum of terms of the form $X_i X_j X_k X_l, X_i X_j X_k^2, X_i X_j^3$ for $i,j,k,l$ distinct. But then by independence and $\expect(X_i) = 0$, $\expect(R) = 0$. Therefore $$ \begin{align*} \expect(S_n^4) &= \sum_{i=1}^{n} \expect(X_i^4) + 6\cdot \frac{1}{2}n(n-1) \expect(X_1^2)^2 \\ &\leq n\expect(X_1^4) + 3n(n-1) \expect(X_1^4) &&\text{by Jensen or Cauchy-Schwarz} \\ &\leq 3n^2 \expect(X_1^4). \end{align*} $$ Hence $$ \begin{align*} \expect\left(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left(\frac{S_n}{n}\right)^4\right) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \expect\left(\frac{S_n^4}{n^4}\right) \leq 3\expect(X_1^4) \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} < \infty \end{align*} $$ as required.
