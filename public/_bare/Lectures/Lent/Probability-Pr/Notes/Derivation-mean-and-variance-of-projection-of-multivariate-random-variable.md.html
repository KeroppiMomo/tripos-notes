For $\v X = (X_1, X_2, \ldots, X_n)$, the expectation is $$ \begin{align*} \expect(\v u^T \v X) &\equiv \big(\expect(u_1 X_1), \expect(u_2 X_2), \ldots, \expect(u_n X_n)\big) \\ &= \v u^T \expect(\v X) &&\text{linearity}\\ &= \v u^T \v \mu. \end{align*} $$ The variance is $$ \begin{align*} \op{var}(\v u^T \v X) &= \expect((\v u^T \v X - \v u^T\v \mu)^2) \\ &= \expect(\v u^T(\v X-\v \mu)(\v X-\v \mu)^T \v u) \\ &= \v u^T \expect((\v X-\v \mu)(\v X-\v \mu)^T) \v u &&\text{linearity} \\ &= \v u^T V \v u. \end{align*} $$
