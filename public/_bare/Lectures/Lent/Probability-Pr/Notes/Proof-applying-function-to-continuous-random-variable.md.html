First consider the case when $\phi$ is increasing. Then $$ \begin{align*} Y \leq y \iff X\leq \psi(y). \end{align*} $$ where $\psi=\phi\inv$. Hence $$ \begin{align*} F_Y(y) = F_X(\psi(y)) \end{align*} $$ and by the chain rule, $$ \begin{align*} f_Y(y) = f_X(x) \dfd xy. \end{align*} $$

Since $\phi'$ never vanishes, there remains the case where $\phi$ is decreasing. Then $$ \begin{align*} Y\leq y \iff X\geq \psi(y) \end{align*} $$ so $$ \begin{align*} F_Y(y) = \prob(X\geq \psi(y)) = 1-F_X(\psi(y)) \end{align*} $$ and a similar argument concludes.
