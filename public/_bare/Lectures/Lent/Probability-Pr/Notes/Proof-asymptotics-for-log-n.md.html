Let $$ L_n = \log n! = \sum_{x=1}^n \log x = \sum_{x=2}^n \log x. $$ Since $\log x\leq \log\lfloor x+1\rfloor\leq\log(n+1)$, $$ \int_1^n\log x\, dx \leq L_n\leq \int_1^{n+1}\log x\,dx $$ $$ n\log n - n + 1 \leq L_n \leq (n+1)\log(n+1) - n $$ $$ \frac{n\log n - n + 1}{n\log n} \leq \frac{L_n}{n\log n} \leq \frac{(n+1)\log(n+1) - n}{n\log n} $$ and since both bounds approach 1 as $n\to\infty$, $L_n\sim n\log n$.
