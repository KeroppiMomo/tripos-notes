Let $X$ be a random variable on $\R$. The moment generating function of $X$ is the function $M_X: \R\to[0,\infty]$ given by $$ \begin{align*} M_X(\lambda) = \expect(e^{\lambda X}). \end{align*} $$

For a random variable on $\R^n$, the moment generating function $M_X: \R^n \to [0,\infty]$ given by $$ \begin{align*} M_X(\lambda) = \expect(e^{\lambda^T X}), \quad \lambda\in\R^n. \end{align*} $$ ($\lambda^T X$ is the dot product)
