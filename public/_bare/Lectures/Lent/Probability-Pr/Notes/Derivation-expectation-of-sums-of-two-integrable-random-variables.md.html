First to see that $X+Y$ is integrable, note that since $|X+Y| \leq |X|+|Y|$, by [[FILE NOT FOUND: this]], $$ \expect|X+Y| \leq \expect(|X| + |Y|) = \expect|X| + \expect|Y| < \infty. $$

The tricky thing is to turn everything into non-negative random variables. Now we have $$ (X^+ - X^-) + (Y^+ - Y^-) = X + Y = (X+Y)^+ - (X+Y)^- $$ so $$ (X+Y)^- + X^+ + Y^+ = (X+Y)^+ + X^- + Y^- $$ where both sides are non-negative random variables.

Hence taking expectation on both sides and applying countable additivity, $$ \expect((X+Y)^-) + \expect(X^+) + \expect(Y^+) = \expect((X+Y)^+) + \expect(X^-) + \expect(Y^-) $$ so $$ \begin{align*} \expect(X+Y) &\equiv \expect((X+Y)^+) - \expect((X+Y)^-) \\ &= \expect(X^+) - \expect(X^-) + \expect(Y^+) - \expect(Y^-) \\ &= \expect(X) + \expect(Y). \end{align*} $$
