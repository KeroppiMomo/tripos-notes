For any $u\in\R^n$. Then $u^T Z = \sum_{i=1}^{n} u_i Z_i$ so the moment generating function $$ \begin{align*} \expect(e^{\lambda u^T Z}) &= \expect\left(\prod_{i=1}^n e^{\lambda u_i Z_i}\right) \\ &= \prod_{i=1}^{n} \expect(e^{\lambda u_i Z_i}) &&\text{independence} \\ &= \prod_{i=1}^{n} e^{\lambda^2 u_i^2/2} \\ &= e^{\lambda^2|u|^2/2} \end{align*} $$ so $u^T Z \sim N(0, |u|^2)$ by uniqueness of moment generating functions. Hence $Z$ is Gaussian.

Clearly $\expect(Z) = (\expect(Z_1), \ldots, \expect(Z_n)) = 0$, and $$ \begin{align*} \op{cov}(Z_i, Z_j) = \begin{cases} 0, &i\neq j &\text{ by independence} \\ 1, &i=j &\text{ since }\op{var}(Z_i) = 1. \end{cases} \implies \op{var}(Z) = I_n. \end{align*} $$
