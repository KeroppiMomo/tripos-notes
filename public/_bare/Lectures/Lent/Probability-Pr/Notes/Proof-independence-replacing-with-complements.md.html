$$\begin{align*} \prob(B) &= \prob((A\cap B)\union(A^\complement\cap B)) \\ &= \prob(A\cap B)+\prob(A^\complement\cap B) \\ &= \prob(A)\prob(B) + \prob(A^\complement\cap B) \\ \prob(A^\complement\cap B) &= (1-\prob(A))\prob(B) \\ &= \prob(A^\complement)\prob(B) \end{align*}$$ as required.

By induction, it can be shown that for any finite sequence of events, replacing some of them with their complements is still independent.

For an infinite sequence, any finite subsequence is independent so the infinite sequence is independent.
