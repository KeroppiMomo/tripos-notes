Since $F_X$ is right-continuous for any random variable $X$, $X$ is a continuous random variable iff $F_X$ is left-continuous, i.e. $\lim_{y\to x^-} F_X(y) = F_X(x).$ This means $$ \begin{align*} \prob(X\leq x) &= \lim_{n\to\infty} F_X\left(x-\frac{1}{n}\right) \\ &= \lim_{n\to \infty} \prob(X\leq x-\frac{1}{n}) \\ &= \prob(X<x) \end{align*} $$ by continuity of probability, so $$ \begin{align*} \prob(X=x) = 0. \end{align*} $$
