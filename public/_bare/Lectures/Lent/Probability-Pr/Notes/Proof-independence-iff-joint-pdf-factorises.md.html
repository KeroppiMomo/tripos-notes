=>: By independence, $$ \begin{align*} \prob(X_1\leq x_1, \ldots, X_n\leq x_n) &= \prod_{i=1}^{n} \prob(X_i \leq x_i) \\ &= \prod_{i=1}^{n} \int_{-\infty}^{x_i} f_i(x_i) \, dx_i \\ &= \int_{-\infty}^{x_1} \ldots \int_{-\infty}^{x_n} \prod_{i=1}^{n} f_i(y_i) \, dy_n \ldots dy_1 \end{align*} $$ as required.

<=: Consider $B=B_1\times\ldots B_n$. Then $$ \begin{align*} \prob(X_1\in B_1, \ldots, X_n\in B_n) &= \prob(\v X\in B) \\ &= \int_{B} f_{\v X}(\v x) \, dx \\ &= \int_{B} \prod_{i=1}^{n} f_i(x_i) \, dx \\ &= \prod_{i=1}^{n} \int_{B_i} f_i(x_i) \, dx_i. \end{align*} $$ For all $j$, by taking $B_i=\R$ for all $i\neq j$, since $\int_{\R} f_i(x_i) \, dx_i = 1$, we have $$ \begin{align*} \prob(X_j\in B_j) = \int_{B_j} f_j(x_j) \, dx_j. \end{align*} $$ Hence $X_i$ has density function $f_i$ for all $i$, and $$ \begin{align*} \prob(X_1\in B_1, \ldots, X_n\in B_n) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} \prob(X_i\in B_i) \end{align*} $$ so $X_1,\ldots, X_n$ are independent.
