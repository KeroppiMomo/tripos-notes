Construct $B_n$ as in the previous proof. Then $$\mathbb P(A_n) = \sum_{k=1}^n \mathbb P(B_k) \to \sum_{k=1}^\infty \mathbb P(B_k) = \mathbb P\Big(\bigcup_n A_n\Big).$$

Set $A_n = C_n^\complement$ to see that $$ \prob(C_n) = 1-\prob(A_n) \to 1-\prob\Big(\bigcup_n A_n\Big) = \prob\Big(\bigcap_n C_n\Big).$$
