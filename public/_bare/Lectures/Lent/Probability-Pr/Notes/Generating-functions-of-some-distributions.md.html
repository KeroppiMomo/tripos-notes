• Bernoulli $X\sim B(1,p)$: $$ G_X(t) = 1-p+pt, \qquad R=\infty. $$

  • Binomial $X\sim B(n,p)$: either using binomial theorem or adding up $n$ independent Bernoulli variables, $$ G_X(t) = (1-p+pt)^n, \qquad R=\infty. $$

  • Geometric $X\sim G(p), p\in(0,1)$, $$ G_X(t) = \fsum n0\infty (1-p)^n pt^n = \frac{p}{1-(1-p)t}, \qquad R=\frac{1}{1-p}.$$

  • Poisson $X\sim P(\lambda), \lambda>0$, $$ G_X(t) = \fsum n0\infty e^{-\lambda} \frac{\lambda^n}{n!} t^n = e^{-\lambda + \lambda t}, \qquad R=\infty. $$
