Take $X_1,X_2,X_3\sim B(1,1/2)$ to be independent random variables. Let $$\begin{align*} Y_1 &= 2X_1-1, & Z_1&=Y_1 X_3, \\ Y_2 &= 2X_2-1, & Z_2&=Y_2 X_3. \end{align*}$$ Then $$\begin{align*} \expect(Z_1) &=\ub{\expect(Y_1)}_0 \expect(X_3) = 0, \\ \expect(Z_2) &= \ub{\expect(Y_2)}_0 \expect(X_3) = 0, \\ \expect(Z_1Z_2) &= \expect(Y_1)\expect(Y_2)\expect({X_3}^2) = 0 \\ \end{align*}$$ and so $\op{cov}(Z_1,Z_2) = 0$.

But $\set{Z_1=0} = \set{Z_2=0} = \set{X_3=0}$, so $$ \prob(Z_1=0, Z_2=0) = \prob(Z_1=0) = \prob(Z_2=0) = \frac12 $$ and hence $\prob(Z_1=0,Z_2=0) \neq \prob(Z_1=0)\prob(Z_2=0)$. Therefore $Z_1$ and $Z_2$ are not independent.
