Assume $\expect(X_1^2) < \infty$. Note that $$ \begin{align*} \expect\left(\frac{S_n}{n}\right) &= \frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^{n} \expect(X_k) \\ &= \mu \\ \var\left(\frac{S_n}{n}\right) &= \frac{1}{n^2} \sum_{k=1}^{n} \var(X_k) &&\text{independence} \\ &= \frac{\sigma^2}{n}. \end{align*} $$ Therefore by Chebyshev's inequality, for all $\epsilon>0$, $$ \begin{align*} \prob\left(\left|\frac{S_n}{n} - \mu \right| \geq \epsilon\right) \leq \frac{\var(S_n/n)}{\epsilon^2} = \frac{\sigma^2}{n\epsilon^2} \to 0. \end{align*} $$
