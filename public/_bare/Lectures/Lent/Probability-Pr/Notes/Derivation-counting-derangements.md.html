Let $\Omega$ be the set of permutations, $A$ be the set of derangements, and $$A_i = \sb{\omega\in\Omega}{\omega(i)=i}$$ for $i=1,2,\ldots,n$. Then $$ A=(A_1\cup A_2\cup\ldots\cup A_n)^\complement. $$

For $i_1<\ldots<i_k$, $$ |A_{i_1}\cap A_{i_2}\cap \ldots \cap A_{i_k}| = |\set{\text{permutations of } \set{1,\ldots,n}\setminus\set{i_1,\ldots,i_k}}| = (n-k)!.$$

Therefore $$\begin{align*} \mathbb P(A)|&=1 - \mathbb P(A_1\cup A_2\cup\ldots\cup A_n) \\ &= 1 - \sum_{k=1}^n (-1)^{k+1} \sum_{1\leq i_1<\ldots<i_k\leq n}\mathbb P(A_{i_1}\cap \ldots \cap A_{i_k}) \\ &= 1 - \fsum k1n (-1)^{k+1}\pmat{n\\k}\frac{(n-k)!}{n!} \\ &= 1 - \fsum k1n \frac{(-1)^{k+1}}{k!} \\ &= \fsum k0n \frac{(-1)^k}{k!}. \end{align*}$$

Interesting this tends to $1/e$ as $n\to\infty$.
