The density function of $Z$ is, due to independence of $Z_1, \ldots, Z_n$, $$ \begin{align*} f_Z(z) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} f_{Z_i}(z_i) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-z_i^2/2} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{(2\pi)^n}} e^{-|z|^2/2}. \end{align*} $$

When $V$ is positive definite, its eigenvalues are all positive. Then $\sigma$ is invertible. So we can invert $$ \begin{align*} x = \sigma z + \mu \iff z = \sigma\inv(x-\mu). \end{align*} $$ The Jacobian is $$ \begin{align*} J = \pdfd{z}{x} = \det \sigma\inv = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\det V}}. \end{align*} $$ By a change of variable, $$ \begin{align*} f_X(x) = f_Z(z) |J| = \frac{1}{\sqrt{(2\pi)^n \det V}} \exp\left(-\frac{1}{2} |z|^2\right). \end{align*} $$ We further find $|z|^2$ in terms of $x$ as $$ \begin{align*} |z|^2 &= z^T z \\ &= (x-\mu)^T (\sigma\inv)^T \sigma\inv (x-\mu) \\ &= (x-\mu)^T V\inv (x-\mu) &&\text{by }V=\sigma\sigma^T. \end{align*} $$ Substituting gives the result.
