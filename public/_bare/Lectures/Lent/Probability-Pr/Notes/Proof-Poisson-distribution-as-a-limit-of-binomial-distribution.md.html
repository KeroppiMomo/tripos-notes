Binomial probability $p_k$ is $$ \begin{align*} p_k &= \pmat{n\\k}\left(\frac\lambda n\right)^k\left(1-\frac\lambda n\right)^{n-k} \\ &= \frac{n(n-1)\ldots(n-k+1)}{n^k} \frac{\lambda^k}{k!} \left(1-\frac\lambda n\right)^{-k} \left(1-\frac\lambda n\right)^n \\ &\to 1\cdot\frac{\lambda^k}{k!}\cdot1\cdot e^{-\lambda} \\ &= e^{-\lambda}\frac{\lambda^k}{k!} \end{align*}$$ which is the Poisson probability.
