For $X\sim P(\lambda)$, $$ \begin{align*} \expect(X) &= \fsum k0\infty k\cdot e^{-\lambda}\frac{\lambda^k}{k!} \\ &=\lambda e^{-\lambda} \fsum k1\infty \frac{\lambda^{k-1}}{(k-1)!} \\ &= \lambda. \end{align*}$$

To find variance, it's often helpful to first compute $$ \begin{align*} \expect\big(X(X-1)\big) &= \fsum k0\infty k(k-1)e^{-\lambda}\frac{\lambda^k}{k!}\\ &= \lambda^2 e^{-\lambda} \fsum k2\infty\frac{\lambda^{k-2}}{(k-2)!} \\ &= \lambda^2. \end{align*} $$ Hence $$\begin{align*} \op{var}(X) &= \expect(X^2) - \expect(X)^2 \\ &= \expect\big(X(X-1)\big) + \expect(X) - \expect(X)^2 \\ &= \lambda^2 + \lambda - \lambda^2 = \lambda. \end{align*}$$
