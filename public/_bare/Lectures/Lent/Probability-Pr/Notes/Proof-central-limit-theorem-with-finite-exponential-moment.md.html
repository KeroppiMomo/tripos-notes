Let $X = X_1$. Assume for some $\delta>0$, $M_{X}(\delta) < \infty$ and $M_X(-\delta)<\infty$.

It suffices to deal with the case $\mu=0$ and $\sigma^2=1$, since otherwise we can consider instead $$ \begin{align*} Y_n = \frac{X_n - \mu}{\sigma} \implies \frac{Y_1 + \ldots + Y_n}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{S_n - \mu n}{\sigma\sqrt{n}}. \end{align*} $$

We will use Taylor's theorem with integral remainder to get the identity $$ \begin{align*} e^{x} = 1+x+\frac{x^2}{2} + R(x), \quad R(x) = \frac{x^3}{2}\int_{0}^{1} (1-t)^2 e^{tx}\, dt. \end{align*} $$ Setting $x=\lambda X$ and taking expectation, $$ \begin{align*} M_X(\lambda) = \expect(e^{\lambda X}) &= 1+\lambda \expect(X) + \frac{\lambda^2 \expect(X^2)}{2} + \expect(R(\lambda x)) \\ &= 1+\frac{\lambda^2}{2} + \expect(R(\lambda x)). \end{align*} $$

It suffices to show that $\expect(R(\lambda X)) = O(|\lambda|^3)$ as $\lambda \to 0$, since then $$ \begin{align*} M_{Z_n}(\lambda) &= \expect(e^{\lambda(X_1+\ldots+X_n)/\sqrt{n}}) \\ &= \left(M_X\left(\frac{\lambda}{\sqrt{n}}\right)\right)^n \\ &= \left(1+\frac{\lambda^2}{n} + O\left(\frac{|\lambda|^3}{n^{3/2}}\right)\right)^{n} \\ &\to e^{\lambda^2/2} = M_{Z}(\lambda) \end{align*} $$ and therefore $Z_n \to Z$ in distribution by the continuity of moment generating function.

Now for $|\lambda|\leq \delta/2$, $$ \begin{align*} |R(\lambda x)| &= \frac{|\lambda x|^3}{2}\int_{0}^{1} (1-t)^2 e^{t\lambda x}\, dt \\ &\leq \frac{|\lambda x|^3}{2} e^{\delta|x|/2}\int_{0}^{1} (1-t)^2\, dt \\ &= \left(\frac{2|\lambda|}{\delta}\right)^3 \ub{\frac{(\delta|x|/2)^3}{3!}}_{\leq e^{\delta|x|/2}} e^{\delta|x|/2} \\ &\leq \left(\frac{2|\lambda|}{\delta}\right)^3 e^{\delta|x|} \\ &\leq \left(\frac{2|\lambda|}{\delta}\right)^3 \left(e^{\delta x} + e^{-\delta x}\right) \end{align*} $$ so $$ \begin{align*} |\expect(R(\lambda X))| \leq \expect\left|R(\lambda X) \right| = \left(\frac{2|\lambda|}{\delta}\right)^3 \ub{(\expect(e^{\delta x}) + \expect(e^{-\delta x}))}_{< \infty} = O(|\lambda|^3). \end{align*} $$
