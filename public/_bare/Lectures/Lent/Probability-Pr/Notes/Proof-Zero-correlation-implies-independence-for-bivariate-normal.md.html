When $\rho=0$, $V$ is a diagonal matrix $$ \begin{align*} V = \pmat{\sigma_1^2 & 0 \\ 0 & \sigma_2^2}. \end{align*} $$ Therefore the density function factorises into $$ \begin{align*} f_X(x) &= \frac{\exp\left(-\frac{1}{2}(x-\mu)^T V\inv (x-\mu)\right)}{\sqrt{(2\pi)^2 \det V}} \\ &= \prod_{k=1}^{2} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma_k^2}} \exp\left(-\frac{(x_k-\mu_k)^2}{2\sigma_k^2}\right) \end{align*} $$ so $X_1,X_2$ are independent $N(\mu_1, \sigma_1^2)$ and $N(\mu_2, \sigma_2^2)$ respectively.
