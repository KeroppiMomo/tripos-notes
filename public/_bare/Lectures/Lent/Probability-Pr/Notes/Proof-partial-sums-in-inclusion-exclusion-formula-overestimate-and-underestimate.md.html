(non-examinable)

We take $f(t) = (1-t)^N$ and use Taylor's theorem $$ f(t) = \fsum j0k \frac{f^{(j)}(0)}{j!} t^j + \int_0^t \frac{f^{(k+1)}(s)}{k!} (t-s)^k\, ds. $$ We can compute $$f^{(j)}(s) = \frac{N!}{(N-j)!}(-1)^j (1-s)^{N-j}.$$ Then for $N\geq 1$ and $t=1$, $$\begin{align*} 0 &= \fsum j0k (-1)^j\pmat{N\\j} + (-1)^{k+1} \frac{N!}{k!\,(N-k-1)!}\int_0^1 (1-s)^{N-k-1}(1-s)^k\, ds \\ &= \fsum j0k (-1)^j\pmat{N\\j} + (-1)^{k+1} \frac{N!}{k!\,(N-k-1)!}\int_0^1 (1-s)^{N-1}\, ds \\ &= \fsum j0k (-1)^j\pmat{N\\j} + (-1)^{k+1} \frac{N!}{k!\,(N-k-1)!}\frac1N \\ &= \fsum j0k (-1)^j\pmat{N\\j} + (-1)^{k+1} \pmat{N-1\\k}. \end{align*}$$

Consider the case when $k$ is odd. Then by noting the above equality and also checking the value when $N=0$, $$ 1_{\set{N=0}} \geq \fsum j0k(-1)^j \pmat{N\\j}. $$ Proceed as in the proof for the inclusion-exclusion formula to get $$ 1-\prob(A_1\cup\ldots\cup A_n) \geq \sum_{j=0}^k (-1)^j \sum_{1\leq i_1<\ldots< i_j\leq n} \prob(A_{i_1}\cap\ldots\cap A_{i_j})$$ and rearrange to get the result.

For $k$ even, replace $\geq$ by $\leq$.
