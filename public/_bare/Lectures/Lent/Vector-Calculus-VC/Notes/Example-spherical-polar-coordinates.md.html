Spherical polar coordinates are defined as $$\begin{align*} x &= r\sin\theta\cos\phi & & & r &\in [0,\infty) \\ y &= r\sin\theta\sin\phi & &\text{with} & \theta &\in [0,\pi] \\ z &= r\cos\theta & & & \phi &\in [0,2\pi). \end{align*}$$ The inverse map is $$\begin{align*} r &= \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}, \\ \tan\theta &= \frac{\sqrt{x^2+y^2}}z, \\ \tan\phi &= \frac yx. \end{align*}$$

The tangent vectors are $$\begin{align*} \v e_r \equiv \vhat r &= (\sin\theta\cos\phi,\sin\theta\sin\phi,\cos\theta), & h_r &= 1, \\ \v e_\theta \equiv \vhat\theta &= (\cos\theta\cos\phi, \cos\theta\sin\phi, -\sin\theta), &h_\theta &= r, \\ \v e_\phi \equiv \vhat \phi &= (-\sin\phi, \cos\phi, 0), & h_\phi &= r\sin\theta. \end{align*}$$

Jacobian $$ \frac{\partial(x,y,z)}{\partial(r,\theta,\phi)} = r^2 \sin\theta. $$

The derivative stuff are $$\begin{align*} \nabla &= \vhat r \pdd r +\frac1r\vhat\theta \pdd\theta + \frac1{r\sin\theta}\vhat \phi\pdd \phi, \\ \nabla f &= \vhat r \pdfd fr +\frac1r\vhat\theta \pdfd f\theta + \frac1{r\sin\theta}\vhat \phi\pdfd f\phi, \\ \nabla\cdot\v F &= \frac1{r^2} \pdfd{(r^2 F_r)}r + \frac1{r\sin\theta} \pdfd{(\sin\theta F_\theta)}\theta + \frac1{r\sin\theta}\pdfd{F_\phi}{\phi} \\ \nabla^2 f &= \frac1{r^2} \pdd r\bigg(r^2\pdfd fr\bigg) + \frac1{r^2\sin\theta}\pdd\theta\bigg(\sin\theta\pdfd f\theta\bigg) + \frac1{r^2\sin^2\theta} \pdfd[2]f\phi. \end{align*}$$
