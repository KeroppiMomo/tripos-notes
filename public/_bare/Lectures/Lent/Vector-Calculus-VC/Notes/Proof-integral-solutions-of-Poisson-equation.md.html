This technique is called the Green's function.

First actually $\tilde\psi(\v x) = \lambda/4\pi r$ solves the Poisson equation $\nabla^2\tilde\psi = -\lambda \, \delta^3(\v x)$ since $$ \begin{align*} \int_{V} \nabla^2\tilde\psi \, dV &= \int_{S} \nabla\tilde\psi\cdot d\v S \\ &= \int_{S} -\frac{\lambda}{4\pi r^2} \vhat r \cdot d\v S \\ &= -\lambda. \end{align*} $$

So $$ \begin{align*} \nabla^2\psi(\v x) &= \frac{1}{4\pi}\int_{V'} \rho(\v x') \nabla^2\left(\frac{1}{|\v x - \v x'|}\right)\, dV' \\ &= \frac{1}{4\pi} \int_{V'} \rho(\v x') \left(-4\pi\,\delta^3(\v x-\v x')\right)\,dV' \\ &= -\rho(\v x). \end{align*} $$
