Let $\psi_1(\v x)$ and $\psi_2(\v x)$ satisfy the Poisson equation with the specified boundary conditions.

Let $\psi = \psi_1 - \psi_2$. Then $\nabla^2 = 0$ with boundary condition either $\psi=0$ or $\v n\cdot\nabla\psi = 0$ on $\partial V$.

Consider $$ \begin{align*} \int_{V} \nabla\cdot(\psi\nabla\psi) \, dV = \int_{V} \nabla V\cdot\nabla V + \psi\ub{\nabla^2\psi}_{0} \, dV = \int_{V} |\nabla\psi|^2 \, dV. \end{align*} $$ But also by divergence theorem, using boundary condition, $$ \begin{align*} \int_{V} \nabla\cdot(\psi\nabla\psi) \, dV = \int_{\partial V} \psi\nabla\psi \cdot d\v S = \int_{\partial V} \psi(\v n\cdot\nabla\psi) \, dS = 0. \end{align*} $$

Since $|\nabla\psi|^2\geq 0$, $\nabla\psi = 0$ everywhere so $\psi$ is a constant.
