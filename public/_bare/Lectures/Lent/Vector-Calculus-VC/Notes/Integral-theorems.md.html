Theorem (divergence theorem) open_in_new

Given a smooth vector field $\v F: \R^3 \to \R^3$, $$ \int_V \nabla\cdot\v F\, dV = \int_{\partial V} \v F\cdot d\v S $$ where $d\v S$ points outwards.

Corollary (divergence theorem for scalar field) open_in_new

For a scalar field $\phi: \R^3\to\R$, $$ \int_V \nabla \phi\, dV = \int_{\partial V} \phi\,d\v S. $$

Proof

Continuity equation open_in_new

Suppose a physical quantity is conserved locally, which means that if the total quantity inside some region $V$ decreases at some rate, then the flux out of the region should be at that rate.

More specifically, if $\rho(\v x,t)$ is the density and $\v J(\v x,t)$ is the current density, $$ \pdd t \int_V \rho\,dV = -\int_{\partial V}\v J\cdot d\v S. $$ Using the divergence theorem, we obtain the continuity equation $$ \pdfd\rho t + \nabla\cdot\v J = 0. $$

Lemma (Green's theorem) open_in_new

Let $P(x,y), Q(x,y)$ be smooth functions on $\R^2$. Then $$ \int_A \Big(\pdfd Qx - \pdfd Py\Big) \, dA = \oint_C P\,dx + Q\,dy $$ where $C=\partial A$ going anti-clockwise.

Proof

If $C=\partial A$ has disconnected components, then the inner boundary should go clockwise.

Theorem (Stokes' theorem) open_in_new

Let $S$ be a smooth surface in $\R^3$ with boundary $C=\partial S$. For a smooth vector field $\v F(\v x)$, $$ \int_S (\nabla\times\v F)\cdot d\v S = \oint_C \v F\cdot d\v x. $$ The orientation is that $d\v x\times d\v S$ should point outward of $S$ (the orientation should be along how you curl your right hand figures around the normal vector).

(Green's theorem is obtained by taking $\v F=(P,Q,0)$ and $S$ be on the $z=0$ plane.)
