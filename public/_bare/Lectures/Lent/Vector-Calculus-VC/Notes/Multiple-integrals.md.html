Definition (area integral) open_in_new

For a region $D\subset \R^2$, define the area integral of a scalar field $\phi:\R^2\to\R$ over $D$ as $$\int_D \phi(\v x)\, dA \equiv \iint_D \phi(\v x)\, dA = \lim_{n\to0} \sum_n \phi(\v x_n)\, \delta A $$ by dividing $D$ into small area elements and adding up the values of $\phi$.

Fubini's theorem states that any way of splitting $\int dA$ always gives the same answer for well-behaved $\phi$ and $D$.

Definition (Jacobian) open_in_new

For functions $(u(x,y), v(x,y))$, the Jacobian is $$ J(u,v) \equiv \pdfd{(x,y)}{(u,v)} = \begin{vmatrix}\partial x/\partial u & \partial x/\partial v \\ \partial y/\partial u & \partial y/\partial v \end{vmatrix}. $$

Proposition (change of variable of area integral) open_in_new

Assume $(x,y)\mapsto (u,v)$ is a smooth and invertible map. The area integral can be written as $$ \int_D dx\,dy\,\phi(x,y) = \int_{D'} du\,dv\,|J(u,v)|\,\phi(u,v) $$ where $D'$ is the image of $D$ and $J(u,v)$ is the Jacobian.

Same thing for a volume integral.
