Definition (surface in R3) open_in_new

Informally, there are two ways to define a surface in $\R^3$:

  • as a level set of a function $F(x,y,z) = 0;$

  • or as a parametrised surface $\v x: \R^2 \to \R^3.$

Normal vector of a surface open_in_new

The normal vector $\v n$ of a surface points perpendicular away from the surface.

  • For the surface $F(\v x) = 0$, $\v n\parallel \nabla F$ (Derivation). Define $$ \v n = \pm \frac1{|\nabla F|} \nabla F. $$

  • For a parametrised surface $\v x(u, v)$, $$ \v n \parallel \pdvd xu \times \pdvd xv. $$

Definition (regular surface) open_in_new

If the normal vector $\v n\neq \v 0$ at all points on the surface, then the surface is regular.

Definition (bounded surface) open_in_new

A surface is bounded if it does not stretch to infinity. Otherwise it is unbounded.

Definition (boundary, closed surface) open_in_new

The boundary of a surface $S$ is a closed curve denoted $\partial S$.

A bounded surface with no boundary is closed.

Definition (orientable surface) open_in_new

If there is a consistent choice of the normal vector which varies smoothly over a surface, then the surface is orientable.
