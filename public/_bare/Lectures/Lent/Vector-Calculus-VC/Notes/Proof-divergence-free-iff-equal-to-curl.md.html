If $\v F = \nabla\times\v A$, then $$ \v F = \epsilon_{ijk}\v e_i \pdfd{A_k}{x^j} $$ so $$ \nabla\cdot\v F = \epsilon_{ijk}\frac{\partial^2 A_k}{\partial x^i\partial x^j} = 0$$ by anti-symmetry.

Conversely, suppose $\nabla\cdot\v F=0$. We'll construct the field $\v A$. Set $A_z = 0$, then $$ \nabla\times\v A = \pmat{-\pdfd{A_y}{z} \\ \pdfd{A_x}{z} \\ \pdfd{A_y}{x} - \pdfd{A_x}{y}}.$$ With reference to this, we set $\v A$ as $$ \v A = \pmat{ \int_0^z F_y(x,y,z')\,dz' \\ \int_0^x F_z(x,y,0)\,dx'-\int_0^z F_x(x,y,z')\,dz' \\ 0}.$$

This way we have $$\begin{align*} (\nabla\times\v A)_x &= F_x(x,y,z) \\ (\nabla\times\v A)_y &= F_y(x,y,z) \\ (\nabla\times\v A)_z &= F_z(x,y,0) - \int_0^z \pdfd{F_x}x(x,y,z')\,dz - \int_0^z \pdfd{F_y}y (x,y,z')\,dz' \\ &= F_z(x,y,0) + \int_0^z \pdfd{F_z}{z}(x,y,z')\,dz' &\text{since }\nabla\cdot\v F=0 \\ &= F_z(x,y,0) + \big[F_z(x,y,z')\big]_0^z \\ &= F_z(x,y,z) \end{align*}$$ as required.
