Divide $V$ into tiny cubes, small enough that we can assume $\v F$ approximately constant on each side.

For the cube at $(x,y,z)$ with size $\delta x, \delta y, \delta z$, the flow of $\v F$ through the $(y,z)$-plane is $$ \big(F_x(x+\delta x, y, z) - F_x(x,y,z)\big) \delta y\,\delta z \approx \pdfd{F_x}x \delta x\,\delta y\,\delta z $$ Do the same for the other sides. Then the total flux through this box is $$ \Big(\pdfd{F_x}x + \pdfd{F_y}y + \pdfd{F_z}z\Big)\delta x \, \delta y\, \delta z = \nabla\cdot\v F\, \delta x\,\delta y\,\delta z.$$

When boxes are combined, the flux through internal faces cancels out, so the total flux exactly accounts for the boundary faces.

So roughly $$ \sum_\text{cubes} \int \v F\cdot d\v S = \int_{\partial V} \v F\cdot d\v S = \sum_\text{cubes}\nabla\cdot\v F\,\delta x\, \delta y\, \delta z = \int_V\nabla\cdot\v F\, dV. $$
