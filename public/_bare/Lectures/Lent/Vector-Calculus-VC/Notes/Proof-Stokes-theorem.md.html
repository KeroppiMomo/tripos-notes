Let $\v x(u,v)$ be the parametrisation of $S$ and $\v x\big(u(t), v(t)\big)$ be the parametrisation of $\partial S$. Let $A$ be the area on the $(u,v)$ plane associated with $S$.

Then $$\begin{align*} \oint_{\partial S} \v F\cdot d\v x = \oint_{\partial A} \v F\cdot\left(\pdvd xu\,du + \pdvd xv\, dv\right) = \oint_{\partial A} \left(\v F\cdot\pdvd xu\right) du+\left(\v F\cdot\pdvd xv\right)dv. \end{align*}$$ To apply Green's theorem, we need to compute $$\begin{align*} \pdd u\left(\v F\cdot\pdvd xv\right) - \pdd v\left(\v F\cdot\pdvd xu\right) &= \pdfd{F_i}{x^j}\pdfd{x^j}u \pdfd{x^i}v + F_i\frac{\partial^2 x_i}{\partial u\partial v} - \pdfd{F_i}{x^j}\pdfd{x^j}v \pdfd{x^i}u - F_i \frac{\partial^2 x_i}{\partial u\partial v}\\ &= (\delta_{ip}\delta_{jq} - \delta_{jp}\delta_{iq})\pdfd{F_i}{x^j} \pdfd{x^p}v\pdfd{x^q}u \\ &= \epsilon_{jik}\epsilon_{qpk} \pdfd{F_i}{x^j} \pdfd{x^p}v\pdfd{x^q}u \\ &= (\nabla\times\v F)\cdot\left(\pdvd xu\times\pdvd xv\right). \end{align*}$$ Hence $$ \oint_{\partial S}\v F\cdot d\v x = \int_A (\nabla\times\v F)\cdot\left(\pdvd xu\times\pdvd xv\right) \,du\,dv = \int_S(\nabla\times\v F)\cdot d\v S.$$
