Forward direction is just a contraction.

For backward direction, take $u_{j_1 \ldots j_q} = c_{j_1} \ldots d_{j_q}$. Then $$ \begin{align*} v_{i_1 \ldots i_p} = T_{i_1 \ldots i_p j_1 \ldots j_q} c_{j_1} \ldots d_{j_q} \end{align*} $$ is a tensor, so $$ \begin{align*} a_{i_1} \ldots b_{i_p} v_{i_1 \ldots i_p} = T_{i_1 \ldots i_p j_1 \ldots j_q} a_{i_1} \ldots b_{i_p} c_{j_1} \ldots d_{j_q} \end{align*} $$ is a number independent of basis.

Since $\v a, \ldots, \v b, \v c, \ldots, \v d$ are arbitrary, $T$ must be a tensor.
