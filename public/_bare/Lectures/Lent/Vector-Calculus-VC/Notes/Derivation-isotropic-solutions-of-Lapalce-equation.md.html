For spherical symmetry, $$ \begin{align*} 0 = \nabla^2 \psi &= \frac{1}{r^2} \pdd r\left(r^2 \pdfd \psi r\right) + \ub{\frac{1}{r^2 \sin\theta}\pdd\theta\left(\sin\theta\pdfd\phi\theta\right) + \frac{1}{r^2\sin^2\theta}\pdfd[2]\psi\phi}_{0} \\ A &= r^2 \pdfd\psi r \\ \psi &= \frac{A}{r} + B. \end{align*} $$

For cylindrical symmetry, $$ \begin{align*} 0 = \nabla^2 \psi &= \frac{1}{\rho} \pdd\rho \left(\rho \pdfd \psi\rho\right) + \ub{\frac{1}{\rho^2} \pdfd[2]\psi\phi + \pdfd[2] \psi z}_{0} \\ A &= \rho \pdfd\psi\rho \\ \psi &= A \log\rho + B. \end{align*} $$
